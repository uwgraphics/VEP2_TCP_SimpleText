BY Lieutenant General Ginckell, Commander in Chief Of Their Majesty's FORCES.
WHEREAS several considerable Sums of Money has from time to time been Issued
out of the Treasury since the Landing of the late Duke of Schomberge unto
diverse Commanders, Enginiers and others on account, for making and repairing of
Fortifications, and other such like Works who have not yet made any account how
the said Money has been Expended, and to the end their Majesty's may receive
full Satisfaction in that particular, I do hereby Order, and Direct, that all
persons whatsoever, who have at any time since the Landing of the said late
Duke of Schomberge in August 1689. Received any Sum or Sums of Money, upon
account for making, or repairing, of Fortifications, Bridges, Passes,
Buildings or any other Work or Service whatsoever that ought to be Accounted
for, do Imediately after Publication hereof deliver in their respective
Accounts of Receipts and Disbursments, with due Vouchers unto the Comissioners
of Accounts in Dublin, in Order to their Adjustments, Whereof all persons
concerned are to take notice accordingly,

Given at the Castle of Dublin, this 4th day of December. 1691. Bar. de
Ginckell, Geo: Clarke.


Dublin, Printed by Andrew Crook Assignee of Benjamin Took, Printer to the
King and Queen at their Majesty's Printing House on Ormonde-Key.
