A CATALOGUE Of Choice Latin and English BOOKS, On Most Subjects:

Which will be sold by Auction, on (...) the (...) of (...) 1694. betwixt Three
and Four of the clock in the Afternoon precisely, at the Sign of the Crown,
near the Pump in Little-Britain.

CATALOGUES may be had gratis, at Mr. Weld's between the Two Temple-Gates,
Fleetstreet, and at Mr. Pero's at the White Swan, next door to the Place of
Sale in Little-Britain.

Conditions of SALE.
I. HE that bids most is the Buyer; but if a Difference arise, the Book or
Books to be re-exposed.

II. That the Buyer not sufficiently known to the Undertaker, give Earnest.

III. That all Persons fetch away their Books within Three Days after the Sale
is ended, paying Ready Money, and likewise Portridge.

Note, The Books are Fair and well Bound, and may be viewed Two Days before the
Sale, in the Afternoon.

There is a Parsel of Pamphlets, and bound Volumes of Tracts, concerning the
Times, which any Gentleman may have the Liberty of choosing what the likes Two
days before the Sale.

Advertisement.
THE Abridgment of Christian Divinity: or, True Religion and Undefiled in all
its Principal and most Useful Branches; both as to Belief and Practice,
briefly stated and explained to the Meanest Capacities. A Work seasonable for
these Times, wherein so many Articles of our Faith are Questioned, and so many
Gross Errors daily published.

Printed for John Pero at the White Swan near the Pump in Little-Britain.
