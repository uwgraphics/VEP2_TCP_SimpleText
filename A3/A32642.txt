A PROCLAMATION Requiring the Members of both Houses of Parliament to give
their Attendance upon the Twenty ninth of August instant.
CHARLES R.

WHereas this present Parliament was Prorogued from the First day of this
instant August, until the Nine and twentieth day of the same Month; And whereas
His Majesty's Pleasure is, That both Houses shall not only meet upon the said
Twenty ninth day of August, but shall continue to Sit for the Dispatch of
diverse weighty Affairs that will be then Proposed and Debated; And His Majesty
being desirous (in respect of the Importance of those Affairs) to have then a
full Assembly of the Members of both Houses of Parliament, Has (with the
Advice of His Privy Council) thought fit to Require, and does hereby Require
and Command all and every the Lords Spiritual and Temporal of this Realm, and
the Knights, Citizens, and Burgesses of the House of Commons, to give their
Attendance at Westminster on the said Twenty ninth day of August instant. And
His Majesty does expect a ready Conformity to this His Royal Will and Pleasure.

Given at Our Court at Whitehall, the Second day of August 1678. And in the
Thirtieth year of Our Reign.
LONDON, Printed by John Bill, Christopher Barker, Thomas Newcomb, and Henry
Hills, Printers to the Kings most Excellent Majesty. 1678.
