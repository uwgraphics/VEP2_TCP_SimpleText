Die Veneris, 16 Martii, 1648.
ORdered by the Commons assembled in Parliament, That no Master or Commander of
any Ship, shall be permitted to Pass or Transport any Seamen or Mariners,
until he shall deliver unto the Captain of the Fort of West-Tilbury a note of
their Names, and shall give security that they shall not act against the
Parliament.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

Die Sabbathi, 14 Aprilis, 1649.
ORdered by the Commons assembled in Parliament, That no Pass or License
whatsoever shall be granted to any person or persons to go beyond the Seas,
until such person or persons shall have first given an engagement under his or
their hands to such person as shall grant the said Pass, that he or they shall
not at any time hereafter engage, act, be ayding, assisting, advising, or
councelling against this Commonwealth; and that no such Pass shall be granted
to any person to take shipping for their Transportation but at a public Port,
and at a public Passage.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

Die Sabbathi, 14 Aprilis, 1649.
ORdered by the Commons assembled in Parliament, That these Orders be forthwith
Printed and Published.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

London, Printed by John Field for Edward Husband, Printer to the Parliament of
England. 1649.
