A PROCLAMATION Requiring the Members of both Houses of Parliament to give
their Attendance upon the Fifteenth day of February next.
CHARLES R.

THe Kings most Excellent Majesty having been pleased to continue this present
Parliament by Prorogation until the Fifteenth day of February now next coming,
with a full purpose and resolution that the Parliament shall be then holden:
And being desirous (for diverse weighty considerations) to have then a full
Assembly of the Members of both Houses of Parliament, has (with the Advice of
His Privy Council) thought fit to declare and publish, and does, by this His
Royal Proclamation, declare and publish his said resolution. And also does
hereby require all and every the Peers of this Realm, and all and every the
Knights, Citizens and Burgesses of the House of Commons, to give their
Attendance at Westminster on the said Fifteenth day of February next precisely;
And His Majesty does expect a ready conformity to this His Royal Will and
Pleasure.

Given at Our Court at Whitehall the Twentieth day of December, in the Eight
and twentieth year of Our Reign, 1676.
LONDON, Printed by the Assigns of John Bill and Christopher Barker, Printers
to the Kings most Excellent Majesty. 1676.
