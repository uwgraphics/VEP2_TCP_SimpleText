A PROCLAMATION For further Proroguing the Parliament.
CHARLES R.

WHereas the Parliament was Prorogued unto the First day of October next
coming, The Kings most Excellent Majesty (by the Advice of His Privy Council)
for diverse weighty Reasons, does by this His Royal Proclamation further
Prorogue the same Parliament, and the same is hereby Prorogued unto the One and
twentieth day of the same Month: And His Majesty does hereby Command and
Require the Lords Spiritual and Temporal, and the Knights, Citizens and
Burgesses, to give their attendance at Westminster on the said One and
twentieth day of October.

Given at Our Court at Whitehall the 25th day of September 1678. In the
Thirtieth year of Our Reign.
LONDON, Printed by John Bill, Christopher Barker, Thomas Newcomb, and Henry
Hills, Printers to the Kings most Excellent Majesty. 1678.
