Liquids, because they melt and lose their force, as l, m, n, r, s.

Double, because they have the force of two Consonants,

j. v.

J

U.

Concords Three.
1.
Whatsoever answers to the question who or what is the Nominative Case to the
Verb, the Substantive to the Adjective, and Antecedent to the Relative; and if
be not one casual word, it is declined like the Noun Invariable Genu, Neuter.
VErbs with their Nominatives true Concord make,
When they the same Number, and Person take.
God blesses

Virtue is commended.

2.
THe Adjective and Substantive embrace,
Confind to equal Gender, Number, Case.
A modest young man.

A rolling stone.

3.
The Relative does not agree in Case, which either comes before the Verb
immdiately, or if any word come between, it is ruled like another Noun, saving
that it keeps his own place, and is governed of the word which follows it.
THe Antecedent and the Relative,
Must the same Gender, Number, Person give.
The man which works, deserves a reward.

They are not your friends, whom you love.
