A CATALOGUE OF Several sorts of Bibles, Testaments, Common Prayers, Singing
Psalms, Book of Homilies, etc. (all Printed at the Theater in Oxford) that are
to be exposed to Sale (by way of Auction or who bids most) in smaller or
greater Numbers, and at such moderate Rates as will be manifestly Beneficial to
the respective Buyers; at the Auction-House in Ave-Mary-Lane over against the
Black-Swan near Ludgate Street on Wednesday the 10, th of December, 1685.

That for the farther Satisfaction and encouragement of the Persons that design
to buy a Book of the several abovementioned sorts of Bibles, Testaments,
Common Prayer, Bound and in Quires, some of each sort shall be exposed to view
for one whole Week, to all Persons that please to come to the abovementioned
Place of Sale.

Conditions of Sale.
I. THAT he who bids most is the Buyer, and if any difference do arise which
the Company cannot decide, the said Book or Books to be exposed again to Sale.
II. That the said Book or Books so Sold are to be paid for and taken away on or
before the 10th day of April, 1686. But for the farther encouragement of the
respective Buyer, such Person or Persons that shall pay for and take away their
Books on or before the 10th of January, 1682/^. following, shall have three
Pounds per Cent. allowed them by way of discount. III. Such Person or Persons
that shall pay for and take away the Books they have bought on or before the
10th day of Feburary following, shall have two Pounds per Cent. allowed them by
way of discount. IV. Such Person or Persons that shall pay for and take away
the Books they have bought on or before the 10th day of March following, shall
have one Pound per Cent. allowed them by way of discount.
After the time no allowance to be made by way of discount.

Note, That there will be a Sale of all sorts of English Books by way of
Auction on the 30 th of this Instant November, 1685. at the within named Place.
Catalogues of which are given by Mr. Not in Pall Mall, Mr. Goodwin in
Fleet Street, Mr. Smith and Mr. Wats in St. Paul's Churchyard, Mr. Southby in
Cornhil, Mr. Ladyman at the Bars without Aldgate, Booksellers, at the several
Coffe-houses in Oxford and Cambridge.
