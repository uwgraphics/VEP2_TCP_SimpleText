A CATALOGUE OF Curious Books, IN Divinity, History, Physic, Mathematics,
Travels, Voyages, etc.


With other SCARCE and VALUABLE BOOKS.

Being the Library of that Learned and Reverend Divine Mr. John Reynolds,
(lately Deceased,)

Will be sold by Auction (or who bids most) at Guild Hall Coffee-House, by
Guild Hall, on Wednesday the Sixth day of this instant December* 1693.
Beginning at Three a Clock in the Afternoon, Exactly, and to continue daily
until all be Sold.

By JOSEPH SHELTON.

Catalogues are distributed Gratis, at Mr. Partridge's at Charing Cross Mr.
Collins at Temple Bar, Mr. Chandlers at the Peacock in the Poultry;
Booksellers, and at the place of Sale.

Also a choice Collection of Pamphlets will be sold in Bundles.

Conditions of Sale,
I. HE who bids most is the Buyer; and if any Difference arise, the said Book
or Books shall be forthwith exposed to Sale again. II. That all the Books in
this Catalogue, not otherwise expressed, (for ought we know) are perfect; but
if any of them appear to be otherwise before they be taken away, the Buyer
shall have his Choice of taking or leaving them. III. That every Bidder shall
advance 6d. each bidding above ten Shillings, and two Pence to Advance in all
Folio's. IV. That all Gentlemen will be pleased to give in their Names and
Places of Abode, after their buying any Book or Books, paying also 5 s. in the
Pound in part of the Book or Books they shall buy (if demanded) and so
proportionably for all lesser Sums; otherwise the Book or Books shall be
exposed to Sale again. V. That the Money for the Book or Books bought, is to be
paid within four days after the Auction is ended, at the place of Sale where
the Books shall be ready to be delivered to them, or their Order. The Buyers
paying Porterage.
