An Excellent COLLECTION OF English Books, Consisting of near Four Hundred
Volumes in Folio, with a proportionable Number in Quarto, Octavo, etc. lately
belonging to Mr. JAMES PARTRIDGE, deceased; together with many considerable
rare Books in Greek and Latin, as well as in the Italian, Spanish, Dutch and
German Languages; most of the large Books Gilt and Lettered.

Particularly here are, Illustriorum Principumque totius Europe Vrbium Tabulae,
ex celeberrima Editione Janssonii, in 8 Vol. nec non Ant. Sanderi Flandria
Illustrata, in a Vol. containing an exact Description and History of all the
Cities in the several Nations of Europe, with accurate Maps of each of them
neatly engraved on large Copper Plates, in 8 Volumes; together with the
Flandria Illustrata of A. Sanders, in 2 Vol. more. In all Ten Noble Volumes,
bound in Velum, gilt Backs and Edges, the Maps all Painted and illuminated very
artificially. Likewise, Fifteen Volumes in Folio of curious Prints and Drawings.

Of which an Auction will be made on Monday the 25th. of this instant November,
and the following days, at Four of the Clock in the Afternoon, at the Naked
Boy, the Corner-Shop of Pater noster-Row and Wawick-Lane; being a very large
and commodious Room for the Entertainment of all Gentlemen-Buyers, etc.

The Books may be Viewed three Days before the time of Sale.

CATALOGUES may be had at Mr. Gilliflower 's in Westminster Hall; at Mr. Wright
's, the Post Office at Charing Cross; Mr. Freeman 's at the Bible in
Fleetstreet; Mr. Keblewhite 's at the Swan in St. Paul's Churchyard; at Mr.
Richard Parker 's in the Portico of the Royal Exchange; and at the Place of
Sale.

Conditions of Sale.
1. THE Highest Bidder is the Buyer, etc.

2. The Books for ought we know are perfect; if any appear otherwise before
they be taken away, the Buyer has his choice of taking or leaving them.

3. That every Person give in his Name and Place of Abode; paying also 5 s. in
the Pound for what he shall buy, and be obliged to take them away, and pay the
remaining part of the Money at the Place of Sale, within three days after it be
ended; the Buyer also paying Porteridge.

4. None to bid less than 6 d. when a Book arises above 10 s.
