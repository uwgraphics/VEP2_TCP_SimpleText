Stephen Langthon, Archbishop of Canterbury, who dyed in the Reign of Henry
III. Ann Dom. 1228. was the first that distinguished the Chapters of the BIBLE
into that order and number as we now use them.

The Compilers of the English COMMON PRAYER Book (as now it is) were, Dr.
Cranmer Archbishop of Canterbury, Martyr. Dr. Goodrick Bishop of Ely, Dr. Skip
Bishop of Hereford, Dr. Thirlby Bishop of Westminster, Dr. Day Bishop of
Chichester, Dr. Holbeck Bishop of Lincoln, Dr. Ridley Bishop of Rochester, and
after Bishop of London, Martyr. Dr. Cox King Edward 's Almoner, Dr. Tailor Dean
of Lincoln, Dr. Heynes Dean of Exceter, Dr. Redman Dean of Westminster, Mr.
Robinson Archdeacon of Leicester.


In May, Anno Dom. 1549* and the third year of the Reign of Edward VI.
