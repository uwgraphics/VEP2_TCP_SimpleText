To the Parliament of the Common wealth of England, Scotland, and Ireland. The
humble Petition of Margare Countess of Worcester.
Shows,
THat your Petioner having been married to Edward now Earl of Worcester in the
year 1639. with a considerable portion, to the value of Twenty Thousand
pounds, and not having received out of the whole Estate, but four hundred
pounds, these nine years, in Lieu of Jointur, Fifts or Thirds. Notwithstanding
her claim and four years attendance: and finding now only Worcester house
unsold and in oursuit to discover some other little thing.

Your petitioner humbly prays your Honors to grant her the benefit of the
said House, and such other things as maybe discovered.

And your petitioner shall ever pray. etc.
