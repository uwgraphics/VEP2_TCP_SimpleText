TO HIS EXCELLENCY The Lord Fleetwood, AND THE General Council of OFFICERS OF
THE ARMIES OF England, Scotland, and Ireland.

The Humble Address of the Inferior Officers and Soldiers of the late Lord
Pride's Regiment.

London, Printed by T. Lock, 1659.

WE having perused a Paper of the sixth of this instant April, set forth by the
Order of the general Council of the Officers of the Armies of England,
Scotland, and Ireland, wherein we find the good old cause, which has cost the
people of these nations so much treasure and blood, in the prosecution of the
war against the late King, to be again asserted.

For which mercy we hope those amongst us that fear the Lord, shall ever praise
his Name.

And because our cousciences bears us witness that we dipped our hands in blood
in that cause, and the blood of many thousands has been shed by our immediate
hands, under your commands in that Quarrel, we are amazed to think of the
account that we must render at the great and terrible day of the lord, if by
our silence the Freedoms of these Nations (so much contended for) should be
loft and returned into the hands of that Family which God has so eminently
appeared against in his many signal providence, little less then miracles.

And therefore in all humility do declare, That we are resolved to stand by you
in the farther prosecution of that good old cause which you mention in the
third page of your representation and Petition to his Highness, and we shall not
think our lives too dear to hazard as formerly, if that old cavalier-party
should appear against you, or any other party which shall endeavor to bring us
into the like thraldom and bondage.
