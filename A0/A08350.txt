The use of this Table following.
IF upon a scale or ruler you divide one inch into 10 equal parts or primes,
and again by diagonal and parallel lines if you subdiuide each of them into 10
equal parts or seconds, as my Trigonal Scales are divided with your compasses
you may take of more exact running measure for boorde and timber then by any
other means whatsoever, and so place the same, or this Table if you will upon
any rule.

Also by means of the column of square roots, you may readily find a square
equal to any paralelipepidon or piece of timber which is thicker then it is
broad. As for example, suppose a piece of Timber to be 10 inches thick, and
29 inches broad: if I multiply those sides one by another, they will produce
290, then seekeing in that column I find 289 the nearest number (...) 290 to
stand against 17: therefore I say 17 inches fere will make a square equal to
such an unlike squared piece: then looking in the column of Timber measure
against 17, you shall find that 5 inches 9 primes or 9/10, and 8 seconds, or
8/100 of an inch in length of that piece will make a foot of Timber. By the
like means you may find how much in length of any breadth of boorde must be in
one foot.

Likewise for boorde measure, suppose for example that a boode appointed to be
measured is 15 inches broad, if I desire to know how much in length thereof
will make a foot. I seek in the columns that stand under the title of unites
and parts for 15, and also over against the same in the column under the title
of board measure, where I find 9 inches 1 prime or tenth of an inch, and 4
seconds or hundreths of an inch will make a foot at that breadth: the like may
be practiced for any other breadth of board whatsoever.
