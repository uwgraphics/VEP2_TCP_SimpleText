The oath of every free Man.
YE shall swear that ye shall be good and true to our souerein Lady Queen
Elizabeth. etc. and to the heirs of our said sovereign Lady the Queen.
Obeisaunt and obedient ye shall be to the Mayor and to the Ministers of this
City. The Fraunchises and customs thereof ye shall maintain, and this City
keep harmless in that that in you is. Ye shall be contributorye to all manner
of charges within this City, as summones, watches, contributions, taskes,
tallages, lot and scot, and all other charges, bering your part as a fre man
ought to do. Ye shall color no forreines goods, whereby the Queen might lose
her customs or aduauntages. Ye shall know no foreign to buy, or sell any
merchandise with any other foreign within the City, or the fra^chise thereof,
but ye shall warn the Chambrelain thereof, or some minister of the chamber Ye shall
emplead or sue no free man out of this city, whiles ye may have right and law
within the same City. Ye shall take none apprentice, but if he be free borne,
that is to say no bond man's son, and for no less term, then for seven
years: within the first year ye shall cause him to be enrolled, and at his
terms end ye shall make him fre of this city (if he have well and truly
served you). Ye shall also keep the Queen's peace in your own person, ye shall
know no gatherings, conuenticles nor conspiracies made against the Queen's
peace, but ye shall warn the Mayor thereof, or let it to your power. All these
points, and articles ye shall well and truly keep, according to the laws and
custom of this City to your power. So God you help, and by the holy
contents of this book.
