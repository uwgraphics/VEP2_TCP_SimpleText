He begins an interlocucyon / with an argument / betwyxt man and woman / and
which of them could prove to be most excellent.





WHen Phoebus reluysa^t / most arde^t was and shene
In the hot summer season / for my solace
Under the vmbre of a tree / both fair and grene
I lay down to rest me / where in this case.
As after ye shall here / a strife there began
Which long did endure / with great argument
Bytwyxte the woman / and also the man
Which of them could prove / to be most excellent.
The man.
The first which I herd: was the man that said
Adam our forfather / by woman's shrewde council
To ete of an apple / was pyteously betrayde
Well happy is he / that with you does not mell.
The woman.
Ihesu of a maiden / and vyrgyn his mother
Was incarnated / to redeem that man had lost
Set thou this one / now against the other
And woman is more excellent / in every cost.
The man.
No women in to angels / never was transformed
But women in to deuyls / full often has been fygurate
For their pride in hell / cruelly to be burned
Unhappy is he / that has one to his make.
The woman.
Many angels to women / have been transported
More than to men / for their chastyte
First our dear lady / thaungell exorted
Saying the son of god / in her conceived should be.
The man.
Joseph by woman / was put in prison
And Vrye was slain / by treason cruel
Dauyd an adulterer / with woman was become
Well happy is he / that with them does not mell.
The woman.
Mankind to deliver / out of this worldly pain
And bring him to the joy / of the celestyall place
God in his mother / took nature humayne
Who dysprayseth women / god send him no grace.
The man.
For woman was slain / the worthy prince Amon
The love of them causes much dyspleasure
By women was dysceyued / the sage Solomon
Which by the mean of them / false ydols did adoure
The woman.
That woman / is most replete with grace
By good reason / I shall prove plain
For god in woman / first took his place
When into this world / he came certain.
The man.
Vyrgyll the sage clerk / of great intellygence
Betrayed was by woman / as written ye may find
Trusting in her grace / in every man's presence
Was tied at a wyndowe / till men did him unbind
The woman.
God to us by woman / paradyse has restored
That before was lost / by our great negligence
And above all saints / hyghly has honored
Mary his mother / woman of most excellence.
The man.
Towns have be destroyed / and cytes many one
Diverse men for women / be dampened deep in hell
More joyful unto man / than woman has be none
Well happy is he / that with them does not mell.
The woman.
By ye mean of Hester / the judgment was respyted
Of Assuerus her husband / for the Jews all
Which should have dyed / had not he delyted
In her hye beautye / and words peticyonall.
The man.
Pryam / Paris and Deiphebus
First by thoccasyon / of women was slain
Also Troylus / Hector and Helenus
And all Troy / has been confounded plain.
The woman.
Iudyth. C. M. men and mo
Made for to fly / and yield in confusyon
After that Holefernus heed / she had cut in two
Which would have dystroyed all the regyon.
The man.
Women their face / and foreheed does paynt
For to deceive / both young and old
The strong Sampson / Dalyda did attaynt
And him betrayed / his heed when she had polde.
The woman.
Woman is honored / in every place
For queen of earth / and of heaven hye
Which is petycioner / for man's trespace
To good on their souls to have mercy.
The man.
Some women can flatter / and most can lie
To obtain man's love / they can feign well
They will never leave it / until they dye
Well happy is he / that with them does not mell.
The woman.
Woman is called / treasure of grace
In heaven our lady / it is full suer
For she for man's soul / does so purchase
That thousands / she saved by her prayer.
The man.
Some that has be taken / for very sage
By woman's love / did so him indeuer
That for it he sold / all his heritage
And after has he dyed / a miserable beggar.
The woman.
Many has offices / and good wages
By women to live / right prosperately
Benefyces / and advantages
To maintain men / accordyngly.
The man.
When a young pegeon / a woman has gotyn
She will make him / at length as strong as a crane
For surely / she will never forsake him
Till that his croper bone wax very lame
The woman.
Then look a cawdell / you do gyt
And soon ye look you / a better norce
For I hard say / god gave man wit
To know the better / from the worse.
The man
Women will speak / and contrary say
They will not shame / a lie to tell
To scolde and brawle / is all their play
Well happy is he / that with them does not mell.
The woman.
The coming / of our lord Ihesu
Many devout woman / did prophecye
Sybellys they were / that it best knew
For to dysprayse women / ye do now vnwyslye.
The man.
One must them give / that they desire
To beat or correct them / they waxen worse
They never will stynt / for to require
As long as a penny / is in ones purse.
The woman.
When all thappostles / from our lord flyed
Women till his death / still did him ensue
Never woman / our lord denied
As Peter did / but ever was true.
The man.
When ye of any man / know the intencyon
Which could be secret / ye publysshe it by and by
Unto your gosseppys / ye make thereof mencyon
As soon as ye together byb / charryng like a pie.
The woman.
Christ after his death / for our consolacyon
First showed / unto woman personally
To Mary magdaleyn / after his resurreccyon
Which for his death / be wayled grievously.
The man.
A Wife of her husband / Will have the audyence
As chyef maystres / her tale to tell
She will cry and weep / except she have the p^emyne^ce
Will happy is he / that with them does not mell.
The woman.
That a man should a woman / or his wife believe
By Abraham / is sygnyfyed plain
For god unto Sara / first knowledge did give
That a child should be procreat: bytwyxt the^ twain
The man.
Many cauteloustrastes / now women they have
To make them proper / and coynte to seem
For proud apparel / ever still they crave
Yet be they most stynkynge / and fylthy within.
The woman.
Of good women / have be found many one
Which have been chaste / and holy vyrgyns pure
And that out of gods laws / never has gone
Wherefore in heaven / now sayntys be they sure.
The man.
But now of evil / there be so many
Their kind increases / still every day
From evil in to worse / so fast they multyplye
That would the devil: had one to carry the other away
The woman.
One vyrgyn another for goddis love has martyred
By compulsyon of man / as scripture has expressed
That in a day.xi. thousand dyed
Wherefore to dysprayse women / ye do not best.
The man.
Consyderyng the evil / which of women have com
How false they be / how cursed and cruel
One ought to fly them / and from them to run
For well happy is he / that with them does not mell
The woman.
All the nobles / of cyme passed
And all the saints / of paradyse
First all from women / they have proceeded
For to dysprayse women / in faith ye be not wise.
The man.
Do weye your blame / and laud in balaunce
To se of them / which twain weye most
And when that ye se / there the dyfferaunce
I trow of your laud / ye will make small boste.
The woman.
For all the blames / and offense
That against us women / ye can lay
Consider the bounte / of our ladies excellence
And all our euylles / her goodness does delay.
La femme replique.
Now the conclusyon / the woman thus began
These infamous men / with all their polecy
All noble women / would gladly defame
Were not our goodness / exceeding their glory.
For Caym killed Abell / his gentle brother
And Judas / Ruben his father did slay
Nero made open / his own mother
To se the place / where that he lay
The men of Jury / made god to dye
Judas scaryot him sold / by false treason
And after him self hanged / this is no lie
Wherefore still in pain / his soul it does won.
Christ's Apostles / did him deny
Men did him crucefye / by great vyolence
And tyrans enraged / dyspyte fullye
Ten thousand / slew of Innocence.
Also in a day / they did confound
A.xi. thousand vyrgyns / unto their death
Some they heedyd / and some they drownde
Some they smolthered / and stopped their breath.
Men they had / perverse courage
As ye may se / now euydently
To do unto saints / such oultrage
Without offense / and no cause why.
Men make wars / and dyscencyons
In every place / as ye may se
Theftes / mordres / and traysons
Replete with all / iniquyte.
More men / there be in prison
In chains bound / for their offense
More women / come unto sermon
To learn goddys laws / with dylygence.
Of every man / in all degree
Mother and norces / women has been
Of all that are / and ever shall be
Women have first thautorite clen.
Wherefore of us / ye ought say well
For to myssay / ye can not wyn
And ye that has no grace / goodness to tell
To hold his peace / is best for him.
Do ye say / of us the best
Syns we ye kept / When ye were small
For of little medlynge / comes much rest
And god amend / the worst of us all.
The auctor.
Of this argument / the hole intent
I marked it / effectually
And after I had herd / them at this descent
I presed toward them / incontynently
But when they saw me / aproche them to
Lest I would repreue / their argument
Full fast they fled / then both me fro
That I ne wist / whyther they went
Wherefore now to judge / which is most excellent
I admyt it / unto this reders prudence
Whyther to man or woman / is more conuenyent
The laud to be given / and wordly magnyfycence.
Finis.
wynkyn de word
