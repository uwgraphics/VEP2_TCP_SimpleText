TO THE READER.
GENTLE Reader, This ensuing Catalogue, has been greatly desired by diverse
friends to be printed alone, to the end, that when any Adversary of the Roman
Church shall demand (as the use of many is to do now adays) a visible
succession of Professors of the Catholic Faith, throughout every Age, all
agreeing in one, and the self same unity of Faith and Belief, from Christ his
time unto ours; thou may easily, and without further seeking of other
Authors, show the same unto him for his satisfaction in this behalf. And if
he cannot show unto thee a like succession of Professors of his Church and
Faith (as infallibly he will never be able to do) all agreeing in the
profession of one and the same Faith and belief from the beginning; then have
thou good reason not only not to credit, or harken unto him, or his Church, but
unto the Catholic, one of whose special Marks, is this of visible
Succession, which of how great importance the same is, and ever has been in the
co^trouersy of Catholic and Protestant belief, I leave to thy prudent
Consideration.

AN ADVERTISEMENT concerning the former Catalogue.
I Would have thee advertised ( Gentle Reader) that in this Catalogue, I have
set down the Names of all the chief Pastors, as being the principal members
of the visible Militant Church, with observation of the year or time, and such
order of succession, as I could find in those Authors, which I had at hand.

Likewise I have set down, the names of some few other Professors, living in,
or near those several ages, in which I have placed them: but in this I have
not been so curious, either to name all which are known, or which my self know
to have been; or to set down any precise year, or orderly succession of their
being; but have contented my self to name only a few in every age, and this
without specifying the particular year, in which they flourished, or observing
any exact order, in which they succeeded one another.

Thirdly, I have set down some principal Counsels, or Assemblies of the
Pastors, and Doctors of the Church, leaving out many others, which I might
have set down. The reason why on the one side, I have set down these
particulers, is, that it may evidently appear, that the Catholic Roman Church
has been continually Visible, in every Age, as the true Church must, and ought
to be.

On the other side, the reason why I have omitted so many other particulers,
is, partly that I may not be too tedious to my Reader; and partly that I may
not seem to exact unreasonable pains to be taken by our Adversaries, when a
like Catalogue shall be required of the^, of the names of like visible
Professors of their Protestant Church and belief throughout every age: which
whiles they do not (as indeed they cannot) I may boldly say with old Irenaeus,
Iren. l. 1. c. 3.


That by only bringing forth that Tradition and Religion, which the greatest,
and most ancient, and to all men manifest, I say, the Church of Rome, founded
and erected by the two most glorious Apostles S. Peter and S. Paul, received of
them, and by succession of Bishops, has brought unto our time; we do counfound
all those, which howsoever, either by vain pleasing of themselves, or vain
glory, or by blindness and perverse judgment, do make erroneous decoctions.

And this I may the better urge, because ( as the same Irenaeus affirms)

there is in the Roman Church a more powerful principality, by reason whereof,
it is necessary for every Church, that is, for all Christians throughout the
whole world, to have recourse unto it etc.

If any be desirous to see more of this matter, let him peruse the Reply it
self (out of which this Catalogue is taken) and the Treatise of Faith, newly
set forth by the same Author.
