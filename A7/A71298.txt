THese are to give Notice, That from the 25th of this Instant June, The Post
will pass thrice a week betwixt England and Ireland, and in like manner betwixt
Dublin and the several Post-Stages in the Country, The two Posts will continue
on the same days they now are, And the third to set out on Thursday Night, and
to return hither on Wednesday Morning in every week. These are farther to
Signify that from the said 25th Instant the Post Office will be kept at the
place where it formerly was in High Street. Whether all persons concerned are
required to bring in their Letters, and dispatches by Eleven of the Clock one
every Post Night,

In Dublin this 15th of June. 1683. George Warburton,
