DIEV ET MON DROIT


By the King.

A Proclamation for obedience to be given to His Majesty's Captain General,
and Officers, in the Disbanding of His Majesty's Army in the Northern parts.
HIs Majesty in His Princely care of the quiet and safety of His Subjects,
that no danger or hurt may happen by any disorder in the Disbanding of any part
of His Majesty's Army in the Northern parts of this Kingdom, does therefore by
the advice of His Parliament, by this His Proclamation declare, That what
person or persons soever shall offend in disobeying the Earl of Holland, His
Majesty's Captain General of His said Army, in Disbanding of any part of the
said Army, or shall disobey any Officer or Officers appointed by the Lord
General to conduct them (being Disarmed) toward their several Counties,
shall be severely punished, as Contemners of His Majesty's Government and
Authority.

Given at the Court at Whitehall this eight and twentieth day of June, in the
seventeenth year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord, CHARLES by the grace of
God, King of England, Scotland, France, and Ireland, Defender of the Faith etc.
God save the King.
Imprinted at London by Robert Barker, Printer to the Kings most excellent
Majesty: And by the Assigns of JOHN BILL 1641.
