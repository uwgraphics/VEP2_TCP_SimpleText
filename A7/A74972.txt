A Proclamation by his Excellency Robert Earl of Essex.
Robert Earl of Essex etc. Captain General of the Army, Employed for the
defense of the Protestant Religion, King, Parliament, and Kingdom.
IT is my Express Will and Pleasure, And I do hereby straightly Charge and
Command all Officers and Soldiers of Horse, Foot, and Dragoones, belonging to
the Army, under my Command, that they and every of them, after Proclamation
hereof made in London, or else where, do forthwith repair to their several
Colors, and Quarters, upon pain of Death.

Given under my Hand and Seal at Arms this Twenty Eight day of February,
1643.
It is His Excellencies pleasure that this Proclamation be forthwith Printed
and Published.

J. Baldwin Secritary to his Excellency.

LONDON, Printed for T. G, 1643.
