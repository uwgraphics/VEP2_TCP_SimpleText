By the King.
A Proclamation for the further Proroguing of the Parliament.
WHereas at Our last Session of Parliament, the same Our Parliament was
Prorogued by Us, until the twentieth day of October then next coming, and now
near at hand; we, by the advice of Our Privy Council, for diverse weighty
reasons Us especially moving, which concern Our affairs both at home and
abroad, do hereby publish and declare Our Royal Will and Pleasure, That the
same Parliament shall be again Prorogued, from the said twentieth day of
October, until the twentieth day of January now next ensuing: whereof the
Lords Spiritual and Temporal, Knights, Citizens, and Burgesses, and all
others whom it may concern, may hereby take notice, and order their affairs
accordingly; We letting them know that We will not at the said twentieth day
of October expect the attendance of any, but such only as being then in, or
about Our Cities of London or Westminster, may attend the making of the said
Prorogation, as heretofore in like case been accustomed.

Given at Our Honor of Hampton Court, the first day of October, in the fourth
year of Our Reign.
Imprinted at London by Bonham Norton, and John Bill, Printers to the Kings
most Excellent Majesty.

ANNO M.DC.XXVIII.
