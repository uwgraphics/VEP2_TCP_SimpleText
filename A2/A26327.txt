A CATALOGUE OF THE Library of Books, LATIN and ENGLISH, Of the Reverend and
Learned Mr. Charles Adams, Late Minister of Great Baddow, near Chelmsford in
Essex: Which will be exposed (to Sale by way of Auction, or Outcry, or who
bids most) at the Black-Boy-Inn in Chelmsford, on Friday the 16th of this
Instant November, 1683.

By the Appointment, and for the Benefit of Mrs. Adams.

Catalogues of which are given gratis to all Clergymen, Gentlemen, and others,
that please to call or send to the Black-Boy-Inn, or to Mr. Hart's Coffee-House
in Chelmsford, or to the Vicaridge-House of Great-Baddow abovementioned. 1683.

To the READER.
THIS Ensuing Catalogue contains the Books of the Reverend Mr. Charles Adams,
late Minister of Great Baddow near Chelmsford in Ess^x, (which if we respect
either their number or quality, are not any way contemptible or inconsiderable)
will be exposed to Sale by way of Auction, or who bids most; a way that has
been encouraged and approved by the Learned and Intelligent Pers^ns in the
City: as having a peculiar tendency to promote Learning and disperse Knowledge;
which considered, its reasonably expected that all Gentlemen and others, but
especially the Clergy, will encourage an Essay of this nature in the Country,
that is manifestly accompanied with such great advantages to their Relicts,
etc. in disposing of their Studies.

This Sale will begin on Friday the 16th of this instant November, 1683. at the
Black* Boy-Inn in Chelmsford, exactly at the hours of Nine in the Morning to
Twel^e, and from One in the Afternoon to Six: wherefore its desired* that all
that cannot be present would be pleased to send their Commissions or Friends
early, lest they miss of the Books they intended, or more particularly desired.

Conditions of Sale.
I. THat he who bids most, is the Buyer; and if any difference arise which the
the Company cannot decide, the said Book or Books shall be forthwith exposed to
Sale again.

II. That the Books in the Catalogue (not otherwise expressed, for ought the
seller knows, are perfect) but if any appear to be otherwise before they be
taken away, the Buyer to have his choice of leaving or taking them.

III. That the Money for the Book or Books so bought, is to be paid by the
Buyer (if not at the time of Sale) within Three Weeks after the Sale is ended,
at the Vicaridge-House of Great Baddow aforesaid; where upon Payment thereof,
the Book or Books shall be ready to be delivered for the time abovementioned.

IV. That the Books may be viewed by any Person from this present distribution
of the Catalogue to the 12th of this Instant, that pleases to repair to the
Vicaridge House at Great Baddow.
