By the King.
A Proclamation for the well ordering of His Majesty's Court and Train, as
well in His present Journey intended toward His Kingdom of Scotland, and
return from thence, as in all other His Majesty's Iourneys and Progresses
hereafter.
THe Kings most Excellent Majesty having taken into his Princely consideration
some inconveniences which may fall out, and happen in His intended Journey to
wards His Kingdom of Scotland, if present care be not had to prevent the
same: Has thought fit by advice of His Privy Council, to publish and declare
by this Proclamation; That His Royal pleasure and commandment is, that none
of His Subjects, of what degree or quality soever they be, during the time of
His Majesty's going, and returning in the said Journey, shall presume within
the Verge of His Majesty's Court, to take up, or possess themselves of any
Lodgings, of their own private authority, but shall first receive a Billet for
the same from His Majesty's Harbingers, or some of them.

And His Majesty being graciously, and prouidently careful to prevent such
grieuances, which may in this Journey happen to His loving Subjects, does
likewise hereby straightly charge and command, that none of His Majesty's
Servants, nor the servants of any Nobleman, or others whatsoever, shall in the
said Journey, presume to take any Cart or Carts, but such as shall be delivered
unto him or them, by someone of His Majesty's Cart-takers, or their Deputies.

And lastly, His Majesty's further will and pleasure is, That this His Royal
Proclamation, and the directions hereby given and commanded, shall likewise
continue, and remain in force for His Majesty's service, and the good of His
Subjects, in all other His Majesty's future Iourneys, and Proycesses
whatsoever. The due observing whereof, His Majesty expects from all His
loving Subjects, as they tender His pleasure, and will avoid the contrary at
their perils.

Given at the Court at Whitehall, the fifth day of May, in the ninth year of
the Reign of our Sovereign Lord CHARLES by the grace of God King of England,
Scotland, France and Ireland, Defender of the Faith, etc.
Imprinted at London by Robert Barker, Printer to the Kings most Excellent
MAJESTY: and by the Assigns of John Bill. M.DC.XXXIII.
