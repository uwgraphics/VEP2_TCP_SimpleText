THe king our sovereign lord calling to his remembrance yt at his last
proliament holden at westminster it was ordained and enacted for the common well of
this his Realm in avoiding ye clyppyng and dystruccyon of his coin / and for
ye preseruacion of good money to be curraunt wtin ye same. That as well all
manner grotes of English coin. and of ye coin of other lands than curraunt
wtin his said Realm for grotes or for.iiij pens being silver and not clypped
though ye same were cracked or worn / should go and be currau^t in all this his
realm for ye so^me yt they were coined for. And also that all grotes as well
English coin / as coin of other lands yt were clypped should not go nor be
curraunt in any wise within his said realm. For asmoche as it is comen to the
perfyte knowledge of his hyghnes / y^ many and great nombre of his subgecte make
themself Ignorant in knowledge of English grotes / and double plakes whyder
they be clypped / or not clypped by mean whereof great trouble and vexacio^
daily is had to his true and well meaning subgectes in making and receyuynge
of their paymentes. wherefore and in avoiding of such vexacion of his said
subgectes. The kings hyghnes by good deliberacion and advise of the lords
spu^^ll and te^porell and other of his counceyll upon the sight of the same
English grotes and double plackes / all be it that many english grotes have
by ye Coyners ben mystryken does declare / decre / and adiuge all English
grot^ and double plackes having ye lymyttes or marks as herafter it is
expressed not to be clypped but to have course and to be curraunt and not to be
refused That is to say. every English grote being silver yt has three
points of ye cross hole on ye side / And the most ^te of ye scripture hole
on the other side to go^ and be currau^t and i^ no wise to be refused though
the same be not ^fytely prynted and coined. Provided always yt this proclamacion
be only exte^ded to such grotes as were coined before ye making of ye said
act of ^lyame^t / and not to grotes new coined sythen the making of the said
act which new grotes shall not be curraunt onles they have their full prynte
on both sides according to the said act.

And also yt every double placke being silver which has his scripture
apparau^t on the one side or on the other side / to go^ and to be curraunt and
not to be refused.

wherefore ye kings hyghnes wylleth and streytely commands that as well all
english grotes as double plackes having the marks or lymytes above said
shall be taken as well by his receyuours as all other ^sones in this his realm

to be currau^t for coin.

And the kyng^ grace for ye more ease and quietness to be had among his said
subgettes in this behalf wylleth and commands yt such money as is
aforesaid reputed for clypped money shall be taken in payment / or in eschau^ge
for iij. (...). and ij. (...). the ounce at ye l^este and in like wise the half
ounce / the quarter and ye half quarter of ye said clypped money after the

proclamacyo^ made in every shyre by any person upon pain of forfeyture by ye
receyuour of the same money so received and upon pain of enprysonement and
fine to be made at the kings will.

And that all and every such person^ and ^sons as shall take or receive
clippyd money by weight furthwith and imediatly in ye presens and sight of him
that shall so v^tur ye same money the said taker and receyuer thereof shall cut
or cause to be cut in sunder all the said clypped money in pain of forfetur^
of all ye said clipped money so received and taken and to have imprisonement

And over that the kyng^ highness straytely commands that none of his

And also the kyng^ highness commands yt all Mayres / baylyfs / constables
/ and all other head officers of cytees / towns / bourghs / or vyllages to se
