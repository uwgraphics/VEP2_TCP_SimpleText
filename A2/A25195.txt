Explanation of the Two LISTS.
Note: THE Number set before any Member's Name, directs to the same Number in
the Alphabet of Plates, to know for what Place that Member serves.

Note: The First Letters of the Members Sirnames are set against every Place
they stand for; and serve, together with the Number set before any Place's
Name, to search in the other Alphabet for the Members chosen for that Place.

For Example.

No 125, Ipswich; Search the same Number in B and P, you will find, No 125.
Samuel Barnardiston, Esquire No 125. Richard Philips, Esquire
