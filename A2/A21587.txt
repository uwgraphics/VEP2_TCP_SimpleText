By the Queen.
THe Queen's hyghnes upon many great considerations for the benfyte of her
common weal, does will and straigtlye command al manner of persons of what
condition soever they be, otherwise then such as be or shall be excusyd by
unfeigned sickness, or licenced by thauctoritie of her Majesty or her
progenitors: to absteyne from kyllyng, dressyng, or eating of any flesh, upon
all such usual fasting days, and namelye the time commonly called the Lent,
upon pain of her Majesty's indignation and imprysonment during her Majesty's
pleasure, and such further fine as therefore shall seem mete.

FOR the better execution whereof, her Majesty charge all Justices of peace,
and all Majors* Bailiffes, and head officers of Cities and Towns corporate,
specially of her Cities of London, and Westmynster, upon proof by two
sufficient witnesses before them had, to commit the offenders thereof to
prison, there to remain during her Majesty's pleasure, and to make fine for
the same accordynglye.

AND finally her Majesty wills and requires all such as shall be upon
necessity and just cause licenced as above is said, that as they regard her
Majesty's favor, they will use the same as modestly and secretly as they may,
both to avoid excess and evil example. Yeuen at our Palayce of Westmynster
the.vij day of February the first year of our Raygne.

God save the Queen.

Imprinted at London by Rychard Judge, and Ihon Cawodde, Prynters to the Queen's
Majesty.

Cum priuilegio Regiae Maiestatis.
