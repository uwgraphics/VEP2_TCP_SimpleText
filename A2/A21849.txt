HONI SOIT QVI MAL Y PENSE royal blazon or coat of arms
By the Queen.
THe Queens most excellent Majesty having ordered that acertaine number of
Soldiers shall be forthwith leuyed and prested to repair into the low
Countries, there to be employed in her Highness service and pay: The charge
of leuying, presting, mustring, arming and transporting of which Soldiers, her
Majesty has by her special Commission, committed to Sir John Norris Knight,
and to such Officers, and other persons whom he shall by writing under his hand
depute in this behalf: Does straightly charge and command, that all such
persons, who have already, or shall receive any pressed money of the said Sir
John Norris, or of such his abouesayd Officers, for this service now committed
unto him, and wherein they are to be used, shall duly observe and keep such
order as shall be given and limited unto them, for the day and time of their
repair to any place, whereunto they shall be by the said Sir John Norris, or
his abouesayd Officers appointed for their meeting, shipping, and
transportation. And if any such person or persons shall to the contrary offend,
or directly or indirectly withdraw themselves after the pressed money by them
received: Her Majesty does hereby notify, that every such person so
offending, and the offense duly proved, shall be punished with death by
Marshall Law. For which purpose, and for the better execution hereof, to the
furderance of this her Majesty's service, Her Highness has been pleased to
appoint peter Crisp gentleman, to be the Provost Marshall: unto whom, in this
service, her Majesty does will and command all Justices, Majors, Sheriffs,
Bailiffes, Constables, Hedboroughes, and all other her Officers, Ministers and
Subjectes, to be ayding, assisting, and furdering, as they and every of them
will answer for the contrary at their uttermost perils.

Given at her Majesty's Manor of S. James, the fourth day of October. 1588 In
the xxx. year of her Majesty's Reign.
Imprinted at London by the Deputies of Christopher Barker, Printer to the
Queens most excellent Majesty
