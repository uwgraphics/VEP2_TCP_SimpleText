By the Queen.
THe Queen's Majesty perceauyng, that notwithstanding the notorious offense of
Thomas Cobham, committed on the Seas, against certain subjects of her good
brother the king of Spain, for apprehention of whom, commandment has been
given to the Ports of the Realm: yet the said Thomas Cobham, as it is
reported, has been secretly in certain places on the land, nere the Sea
side, and not apprehended. For remedy hereof, and for a direct and speedy
proceadyng in Justice against him: Wills and commands all manner of
persons, of what condition soever they be, to do their uttermost to apprenhende
by Sea or by land the said Thomas, or any of his accomplices. And if any person
shall harbor, or any wise directly or indirectly, succor or relieve him, or
any of his accomplices, or shall forbear to do their endeavor to stay him, or
any of them, whersoeuer he or they may reasonably be stayed: her Majesty will,
that such persons for such succouring of him, or forbearyng to do their
endeavor to apprehende him or his accomplices, shall be taken and ordered as
accessories to pyracie.

Yeuen at Grenewiche, the.xxi. of July, the sixth year of her Majesty's most
noble Reign.

God save the Queen.

Imprinted at London by Richard Judge and John Cawood, printers to the Queen's
Majesty.
