TO all Christian people to whom this present writing shall come, We William
Elliots and Matthias Meisey of London Gentlemen, send greeting in our Lord God
everlasting.
WHEREAS our most gracious Sovereign Lord King JAMES, by his Highness Letters
Patents under the great Seal of England, bearing date the tenth day of August
last past before the date of these presents, upon consideration of the rent of
two hundred ninety three pounds sixteen shillings eight pence, in liew of
Customs and Imposts heretofore answered unto his Majesty for Steel, imported
into this Realm of England reserved, and upon other rent and considerations in
the said Letters Patents expressed, Has been pleased therein to express his
gracious pleasure that his Subjects of England and Ireland, should be served
with Steel made after a new manner or invention, found out by us the said
William Elliots and Matthias Meisey of making of Steel, and then made within
his own Dominions; And that all importation of Steel made in foreign parts
should be forborne after the five and twentieth day of March, then next
ensuing, under the forfeiture of the said Steel and diverse other forfeitures
and penalties, with power and authority given therein, unto us, our executours,
administrators, deputies, factors, servants, and assigns to enquire of, and
examine any Masters or owners of Ships, Barks, or Lighters, and Mariners, of
any Steel brought in contrary to his Majesty's prohibition, and also with a
Constable of the place or Officer of his Majesty's Customs, at our pleasure to
go aboard, view, search, and survey any Ship, Vessel, or Bottom, Cellar,
Vault, Warchouse, Shop, or other place, and open any Cases, Caskes, Chests,
Fattes, or any bulk whatsoever, where any steel shall be suspected to be
laid up, packed, or concealed: And also to seize and make confiscate, and to
take and carry away all such steel so imported, contrary to his Majesty's
Inhibition: The one moiety whereof is granted by his Majesty to us the said
William Elliots and Matthias Meisey, and the other moiety reserved to his
Majesty's own use and benefit: Straightly commanding all Customers,
Collectors, Comptrollers, Searchers, Surueyours, Waiters, or Officers of the
Custom, and all Officers and Ministers of the Admiraltie, and all other
Officers, to be aiding and assisting, to us, our executors, administrators,
deputies, factors, servants, and assigns for the enjoying of his Majesty's
grant, and further performance of his Majesty's service, as in and by the said
Letters Patents, more at large it does and may appear. Now know ye, that we
the said William Elliots and Matthias Meisey, by virtue of the said Letters
Patents, and the authority therein and thereby unto us granted, as aforesaid,
Have constituted, authorized, and appointed, and by these presents do
authorize, constitute, and appoint (...) to be our sufficient and lawful
deputy and factor, during our pleasures, for the due execution of the said
Letters Patents, and all powers, liberties, and authorities therein, and
thereby granted, as aforesaid: Giving, and by these presents granting, unto the
said (...) full and free license, liberty, power, and authority to see all
entries made into the Custom house of goods imported, and to require and take
copies thereof, and to enquire of and examine any master, owner, or mariner of
any ship, bark, or lighter, of any steel imported, contrary to his Majesty's
prohibition: And also with a Constable of the place or other Officer of his
Majesty's customs, to go aboard, view, and search any ship, vessel, or
bottom, cellar, vault, warehouse, shop, or other place: And to open any cases,
caskes, chests, fattes, or any bulk whatsoever, where any steel shall be
suspected to be laid up, packed, or concealed, forbidden as aforesaid. And also
to seize, take, and carry away all such steel, so imported contrary to his
Majesty's Inhibition: And to require the help and assistance as aforesaid, of
all Officers and ministers for his Majesty's service expressed: And to execute,
follow, and effect, all and every other thing and things, whatsoever meet and
requisite, in, about, or concerning the due execution and accomplishment of the
premises, in as large and ample manner and form, as we the said William
Elliots and Matthias Meisey may, might, or could do in proper person, by
virtue of the said Letters Patents, or any thing therein contained: Ratifying,
allowing, and confirming all and whatsoever the said (...) shall do or cause
to be done by virtue of these presents, in, about, or concerning the premises,
or any of them: Praying, and requiring in his Majesty's name, by virtue of the
said Letters Patents, and the authority therein and thereby, unto us granted,
as aforesaid, All Majors, Sheriffs, Justices of Peace, Bay liffes of
Liberties, Constables, and all other his Highness officers and ministers, and
all other his Majesty's loving Subjects whatsoever, to be from time to time,
aiding and assisting unto the said (...) in the due execution of this service,
according to his Majesty's pleasure in the said Letters Patents expressed.
Provided that the said (...) do not conniue or consent to the coming in of
any steel, contrary to his Majesty's prohibition, upon such pain and
penalties as shall be due to those that contemn his Majesty's prerogative, and
upon such other pain as the Patentees may by course of Law inflict upon them.
In witness whereof we the said William Elliots and Matthias Meisey, have
hereunto put our hands and seals.

Yeouen the xxvij. day of March, in the year of the Reign of our Sovereign
Lord James by the grace of God of England, France and Ireland, King Defender of
the Faith etc. the fifteenth, and of Scotland the fiftieth.
