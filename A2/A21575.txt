By the King and the Queen.
THE QVENES Majesty being informed that a number of gentlemen, and others of
good hauor, having dwelling places, and mansion houses, in diverse Shires of
the Realm (whose abode there, specially at this time of service, should be
requisite and necessary) have withdrawn them selfs from thence, and lie here
in or about the city of London. Do the therefore strayghtlye charge and
command, that al and every such gentlemen, shall before the.xx. day of Apryll
next, depart home unto their dwellynges and mansion houses, being in the said
Shires, and there to put them selfs in a redynes to serve the queens hyghnes,
and the country, as need and occasion shall require, onles they be such as
remain here by special order and commandment, upon pain of imprisonment,
and further fine at her hyghnes pleasure. Dated at her majesty's manor of
Grenewhich the.xxx. day of March.

God save the King and the Queen.
EXCVSVM LONDINI IN AEDIBVS Iohannis Cavvodi, Tipographi Regiae Maiestatis.

Anno. M. D. LVIII.

Cum priuilegio ad imprimendum solum.
