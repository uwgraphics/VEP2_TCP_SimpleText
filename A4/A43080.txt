An Exact TABLE of the Weight of Gold and Silver.
CONTAINING, I. The Value of any Quantity of Silver, from 1 Grain to 200000
Ounces, at 5 s. 2 d. 5 s. 4 d. 5 s. 6 d. and 5 s. 8 d. per Ounce Troy. II. The
Value of any Quantity of Gold, from 1 Grain to 40 Pound Weight, at 4 l. 2 s.
per Ounce, may be known by Inspection. III. The True Weight that any Sum of
Our New Milled Money ought to Weigh, according to the Standard Rate of 62
Shillings, being a Pound Troy for Weight, and 11 Ounces 2 Peny-weight for
Fineness: As also, how much Silver, at 5 s. 2 d. per Ounce, will pay any Sum
under 1000 l. IV. The Weight of a True Guinea; 22 Shillings, or 20 Shillings
Broad Gold; as also the French and Spanish Pistole.
Adapted to the Use of such as deal in Plate or Money, and may (at this time)
be useful for the whole Nation to set up in their Studies, Shops, Warehouses,
or Compting-houses.

Calculated by EDW. HATTON, Gent. Author of The Merchant's Magazine.

The Construction and Use of this TABLE.
1. To find the Value of any quantity of Silver, as Hammered Money, etc. Admit
you would know the value of 89 Ounces of Silver at 5 s. 2 d. per Ounce. Look in
the Column of Ounces, and against 89 under 5 s. 2 d. per Ounce you will find 22
l. 19 s. 10 d. to be the value thereof. Or if your Silver is at 5 s. 4 d. etc.
look under the Column having your price of an Ounce at the Head, and against
your number of Ounces you have the true value thereof. Or if your Quantity is
946 Ounces, 19 p. w. 15 Grains, at 5 s. 8 d. per Ounce, lb. s. d. q. against
900 Ounces you have (under 5 s 8 d.) 255 00 00 against 46 Ounces you have
(under 5 s. 8 d.) 13 00 08 against 19 p. weight 0 05 04 2 against 15 Grains
(viz. 8 and 7) is 0 00 02 0 The Sum of which is the Answer, or 268 06 02 2

Example the 2d. Admit you would know what 564 l. 10 s. 6 d. of Milled Money
ought to weigh? Answer. In the Column under Milled Money, the weight of lb.
p.w. gr. 500 l. is 161 03 09 16 60 l. is 19 04 05 03 4 l. is 1 03 09 16 10 s. 6
d. is 0 02 00 16 The Sum of which is the Answer 182 01 05 03

Example 3d. To find the value of any Quantity of Gold; as suppose you would

Right Hand, and under lb. s. d. 10 Ounces you have 41 00 00 10 Penny-weight 2
01 00 10 Grains 0 01 08 The Sum of which, for Answer, is 43 02 08
