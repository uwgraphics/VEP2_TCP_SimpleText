An Advertisement touching this Book.
THis little Vocabulary is an extract only of such words as I used to Note in
the larger, to be as certain Way-marks unto Childrens memories, for the better
learning of the rest; and to save my own labor, and the Childrens Books
somewhat, I have caused them to be here printed by themselves, as having their
proper end; which is, to teach little ones how to call those things in Latin,
which are every way obvious unto them; and whose names they know in English.
And this we may observe to be the first step toward the gaining of any
language: what farther use may be made of it, I leave to his discretion that
will teach it; only let me tell the new beginner, that for his help in
pronouncing Latin words, I have marked the syllable thus (') wherein he must
lift up the sound above the rest: and to hint him a little toward his Grammar,
I have caused the ending of the Genitive Case of every word to be set down, and
the letter denoting its Gender, viz. m. the Masculine, f. the Faeminine, n. the
Neuter. c. 2. the Common of two, and d. g. the Doubtful Gender.

And now that I have dug deep enough (as I conceive) for the laying of a
sure foundation of the Latin Tongue in a Child that can (though but
indif^^rently) read English, I will hasten what I can (at by times) in works
that come nearer to perfection. Which that I may cheerfully effect, God
graciously vouchsafe his assistance as hitherto.
