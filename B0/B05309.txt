ACT Continuing the Importation of Irish Meal and Oats, and allowing Bear to be
Imported until the fifteen Day of May next.
Edinburgh, February 25th. 1696.
THe Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council, being Informed that the Prices of
Meal, Oats and Bear in the Western Shires of this Kingdom, does still continue
above the Rates set down in the Act of Parliament One Thousand Six Hundred and
Seventy Two: Do therefore according to the Power given to them by the foresaid
Act, allow the Importation of Meal, Oats and Bear (but of no other Grain) from
Ireland, to any Port or part betwixt the Mouth of Annand, and the Head of
Kintyre, and that until the Fifteen Day of May next to come Inclusive: And
therefore, Do hereby Suspend all Proclamations and Commissions made for
Restraining the said Importation during the said Time and Space of Permission,
but prejudice always to the Prohibition contained in the foresaid Act, after
the said Fifteen Day of May next Inclusive And like ways, as to all the other
Ports and Parts of this Kingdom not hereby Privileged, as if this Allowance
had not been Granted; And Ordains these Presents to be Published and Printed,

Extracted by me GILB. ELIOT, Cls. Sti. Concilii.

Edinburgh, Printed by the Heirs and Successors of Andrew Anderson, Printer to
