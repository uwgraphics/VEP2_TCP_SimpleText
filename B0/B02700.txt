A CATALOGUE Of Excellent and Rare BOOKS, To be Sold by way of AUCTION the
24th. day of July. Which was the Library of the late Lord Dirltoun sometime
Advocate to His Majesty Deceased. The BOOKS are to be seen Four days before
the Auction being at the Place where they are to be Sold, to wit, at
Pitcairlie's Stone Land at the Cross, third Door of the Turnpyke on the
Right Hand. The Catalogues are to be had at Mr. Charles Lumisden's Shop in the
Locken-Booths, The time of the Sale, is only in the Afternoon, and begins at
Two, and ends at Six; and will continue every day in the Week until the Books
be Sold off.

EDINBVRGH, Printed by George Mosman at his Printing house in Harts-Closs over
against the Trone-Church, Anno Domini 1690.

The CONDITIONS of the Sale are these.
1. He who bids most is the Buyer.

2. If any difference arise which the Company cannot decide, the Books shall be
forthwith Exposed to Sale again.

3. He who pays not presently, is to give Earnest Money to secure the Books
for him, against he call for them.

ADVERTISEMENT.
WHat Books shall happen to be unsold at the Auction, are to be had at the
place aforesaid at any hour.
