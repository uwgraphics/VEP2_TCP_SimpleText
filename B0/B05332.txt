ACT of COUNCIL, Dispensing with Circuit Courts for this Instant Year 1685.
Edinburgh, the 29th day of April 1685.
FOrasmuch as His Majesty's high Commissioner, and the Lords of Privy Council,
having thought fit (in regard his Majesty's Service does not now require that
Circuit Courts should be held for this year, as is appointed by Act of
Parliament) To dispense therewith; They, to the effect notice hereof may be
given to all persons concerned, Do hereby Ordain the Macers of Council, to pass
to the Market Cross of Edinburgh, and there, by open Proclamation, make
publication that the said Circuit Courts are not to be kept this year, that
none pretend ignorance. Extracted forth of the Records of Privy Council, by me

COL. MACKENIE, Cls. Sti. Concilij.

Edinburgh, Printed by the Heir of Andrew Anderson, Printer to His most Sacred
Majesty, Anno Dom. 1685.
