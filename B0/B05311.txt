ACT Discharging any Person to go Aboard of, or Correspond with French
Privateers.
Edinburgh, August 3, 1697.
THE Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council being informed, that notwithstanding
the Laws and Acts of Parliament against such as Correspond with His Majesty's
Enemies; and particularly the Act One thousand six hundred ninety three,
Entitled Act against Corresponding with France: Yet upon the occasion of
French Privateers, and others His Majesty's Enemies coming upon the Coasts of
this Kingdom, several Persons have either been ensnared, or have presumed to go
on Board of them, or otherways to Correspond with them, albeit Enemies, and in
actual Hostility as said is: Therefore the said Lords of Privy Council have
discharged, and hereby discharge all and every one of His Majesty's Leidges to
go on Board any of the said Privateers, or otherways to Correspond with them
in any sort, or to have any manner of dealing with them, without express
License obtained for that effect from the said Lords of Privy Council, under
the pains in the said Acts. And the said Lords of Privy Council do hereby
Ordain, that all Sheriffs, Stewarts, Baillies and their Deputs, and other
Magistrates whatsoever, be careful that these presents be duly observed, as
they will be answerable: As also, that they be Printed and Published at the
Mercat Cross of Edinburgh, and other places needful.

Per Actum Dominorum Secreti Concilii. GILB. ELIOT Cls. Sti. Concilii.

Edinburgh, Printed by the Heirs and Successors of Andrew Anderson, Printer to
the King's most Excellent Majesty, Anno DOM. 1697.
