ACT of Privy Council, Anent the Punishment of these who refuse to Serve in the
Foot-Militia.
Edinburgh, the 25. of October, 1688.
THe Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council being informed, that some persons who
are pitched upon by the Leaders and Out-reikers of the Foot-Militia, refuse the
said Service, upon the ordinary and accustomed Pay; Do hereby Require and
Authorize, the Justices of Peace, Commissioners of Assesment, or the Militia,
to Seize upon any person so Refusing, and to Imprison them in the next
convenient Prison, and to Fine them in the Sum of Twenty pounds Scots, and to
inflict upon them such Corporal Punishments as they shall think fit, by
Whipping or otherways. For doing whereof, these Presents shall be their
Warrant. Extracted forth of the Records of Privy Council by me

COLIN McKENIE, Cls. Sti. Concilii.

IDINBURGH, Printed by the Heir of Andrew Anderson, Printer to His most Sacred
Majesty, Anno DOMINI. 1688.
