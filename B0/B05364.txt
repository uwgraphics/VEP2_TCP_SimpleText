EDINBURGH, The eighteenth day of February, one thousand six hundred and sixty
four.
FOrasmuch as it has pleased the Kings Majesty, by a Letter, dated at
Whitehall the sirth of this instant, directed to His Majesty's Privy Council;
bearing, that whereas, His Majesty, by a Letter of the date the twenty third
day of January, the year of GOD, one thousand six hundred and sixty three, did
appoint a Proclamation to be issued forth, suspending the payment of the Fines,
imposed by an Act of the second Session of the last Parliament, entitled,
Anent persons excepted forth of the Act of Indemnity, dated at Edinburgh the
ninth of September, in the year of GOD foresaid, until His Majesty should
declare His further pleasure, suspending also the penalties for not payment in
the interim; therefore to appoint a new Proclamation to be issued in His
Majesty's name, requiring and commanding all such fined persons, who shall be
charged in name of the Lord Thesaurer, Thesaurer-depute or Advocat, betwixt the
date hereof and the first day of Angust, in this instant year of GOD, one
thousand six hundred and sixty four, to make payment to such as His Majesty
shall authorize, of the first half of the Fines imposed by the Act of
Parliament, betwixt and the first term of Martimass next thereafter, under the
pains and penalties contained in the said Act; as also, those same persons who
shall be charged to pay the second moiety at or before the term of Candlemass
next following, in the year, one thousand six hundred and sixty five, under the
pains contained in the said Act. Therefore, the Lords of His Majesty's Privy
Council, in obedience of His Majesty's commands, ordains a Macer to make
intimation of His Majesty's Royal pleasure foresaid, concerning the fined
persons by the said Act of Parliament, by open Proclamation at the Mercat Cross
of Edinburgh, requiring and commanding, in His Majesty's name, all persons
concerned, to give obedience as they will be answerable, under all highest
pains; and declares the same to be as sufficient, as if Proclamation were made
at the Mercat-crosses of the head Burghs of the several Shires of this Kingdom.
And ordains these presents to be printed, and published in manner foresaid, that
none pretend ignorance.

Pet. Wedderburne, Cl.Sti Concilii.

EDINBVRGH, Printed by Evan Tyler, Printer to the King's most Excellent
Majesty, 1664.
