TO HER MAJESTY OF BOHEMIA.
MOST gracious Soueriange,
Lick as in the time of sickness, it is a misery, to be wholly destituit of
Physicians; Also is it in the day of heaviness (Zepha. 1.15. of anguist Ier.
30.7. of tribulation 2. Esdr. 16. verse. 66.) of trouble Esaias 22. 5. To have no

streched out my hands, and there was none, to comfort me. Lam. 1.17. They have
heard, that I morned, but there is none to comfort me verse. 21.

Nevertheles the childeren of God sometimes want ontwarde comfort, yet they
never want the comfort of the Lord there God; For his mercy, comfort them.
Psalm. 119.76. In like manner has the Lord in former time comforted his People,
and he has redeemed Jerusalem. Esaias. 52.9. He is the Lord God, and in that

mother comfort, so will I comfort you, and ye shall be comforted. Esaias
66.13. Therefore most Gracious Queen, I am assured that your Majesty in this
(the which is the highest and most excelent comfort) can have no want: for your
Majesty keeps the ways of the Lord, neither do you wickedly against your
God. All his laws are before you, and you cast not his commandments from you.
Psalm. 18.21.22. Neither wants your Mtie for the present Godly comforters, the
which say unto your Mtie. Be you strong, fear not. Esaias 35.4. The Lord is
with you. Judge. 6.12. Behold your God comes, he will save you. Esaias 35.4.
Nevertheless that Christian feelinge which I have suffered concerning the
lose of your Mties son Fridericus (for it touched my heart) forces me in this
Dialogue te comfort your Mtie 2. Cor. 1.4. The which I have also done, in six
Orations in Latin, dedicated unto his most Royal Mtie the King of Bohemia,
your Lord. I hope it will please your Mtie, according unto your accostomed
goodness, and gracious favor, to except this my on desired service. Which
althoug' it may seem on timely, yet I have till this present deferred the
same, according to the rules of the best Physicians, who in a vehement flux
of Phlegma praescribe not a present remedy, but much rather use outward
application, sufficient to digest the Humor: Alsoo must we be silent when the
grief is fresh, till the grieved in process of time be more apt to admit true
and touchingh Consolation. Therefore I most humbly beseech your gracious
Majesty, to take it so: Which being done, it will bring great joye unto me,
and shall always remain your Mties most affectioned servant, who will not
cease continually to pray unto God, for your Mtie, and yours. Ephesians. 1.16. In
Haerlem.

Your Majesty's Most humble Servant, DANIEL SOVTERIVS Minister of Gods Word,
in the Nederduch Church, in Haerlem.
