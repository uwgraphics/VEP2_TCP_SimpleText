ACT Of the Committee of His Majesty's Privy Council, against the Exporting of
the Current Coin.
Edinburgh March 15. 1695.
THe Committee of His Majesty's Privy Council, about the Export of Money; To
Macers and Pursevants, these are to Warrant and Command you, to go immediately
to the Mercat Cross of Edinburgh, and there by sound of Trumpet, and open
Proclamation, make Intimation to all Merchants, Factors, Exchangers, or others
that may be concerned, and who have put Aboard either by themselves or others,
any quantity of Money above what the Law allows, of the Fleet or Ships now
presently in the Harbor of Leith, or the Road thereof, or any other Road or
Port, to be Transported forth of this Kingdom, whether in Gold or Silver; that
they immediately bring the same a Shore, to remain within this Kingdom, with
Certification that if they fail therein, they shall be called particularly
before the Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council, as unlawful Transporters of
Money: and the same being proven either by Oath of Party, or otherways; they
shall be punished conform to the Acts of Parliament, with all rigor. And
Ordains these Presents to be printed.

Per Actum Dominorum Secreti Concilii. GILB. ELIOT Cls. Sti. Concilii.
WILLIAM.
Edinburgh, Printed by the Heirs and Successors of Andrew Anderson, Printer to
His Most Excellent Majesty. Anno Dom. 1695.
