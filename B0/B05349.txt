ACT Taking off the Restraint put upon the Export, and Discharging the Import
of Victual into this Kingdom.
Edinburgh, The 24, of June, 1685.
FOrasmuch as the Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council, having by their Act and
Proclamation of the twenty fifth day of May last bypast, Prohibited and
Discharged the Exporting of any Victual forth of this Kingdom, until further
Order, for the Reasons therein mentioned; and by another Act of the same date
allowed the Commissaries for the Army to import from Ireland five thousand
Bolls of Meal. And the Council now finding it convenient that the said
Restraint should be taken off, and the Importing of the said Meal or any other
Victual from Abroad be Discharged. They therefore hereby Warrant and Allow the
Exporting of all Grains of Victual forth of this Kingdom to any Place Abroad,
notwithstanding of the said Prohibition. And further, Do hereby strictly
Prohibit and Discharge the Importing of the said five thousand Bolls of Meal
from Ireland, or any Victual from any other Place Abroad to this Kingdom, under
the Pains and Certifications contained in the Laws and Acts of Parliament made
thereanent. And Ordains all Collectors, Waiters, and Customers to see these
Presents punctually Obeyed, as they will answer at their Peril. And the same to
be Printed and Published, that none pretend Ignorance.

Extracted forth of the Records of Privy Council, by me. WILL. PATERSON, Cls.
Sti. Concilii

Edinburgh, Printed by the Heir of Andrew Anderson, Printer to His most Sacred
Majesty, Anno DOM. 1685.
