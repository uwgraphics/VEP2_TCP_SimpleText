There be three manner of rentys that is to sey rent service rent charge and rent
sek rent service is where a man holdyth of a another by fealte and for to do
suyt to his court and yeldynge to him a certain rent by the year for all manner
of seruyces.

And note well that if the lord be seysed of the service and rent beforesayd
and they be behynd and he dystrayne and the tenant rescow the distress he may
have assise or a wryt of rescous / but it is more necessary for him to have
assise than a wryt of rescouse / for so much that by assise he shall recover
his rent and his damagis / but by a wryt of rescous he shall not recover but
the thing and the damagis.

And note well that if ye lord be not seisid of the rent and service and they
be behynd and he dystrayn for them and the tenant take again the dystresse /
he shall not have assise but a wryt of rescous.

And note well that if the lord distrayn his tenant in socage for knyghtys
service which is not with said of him and a vow for the same service i^ court
of record he shall be chargyd by the same service / per

ffynch termino hillare anno. xlvi.

And note well that if the lord may not find a distress by. ii. year / he
shall have agayns the tenant a wryt of cessauit per biennium / as it aperith
by the statute of westminster. ii. capitilo. xxi. And if ye tenant dye the same time
and his issue enter / the lord shall have agayns the issue a wryt sur cessaunt
/ or if the tenant alayn / the lord shall have agayns the alieue the foresaid
wryt But if the lord have issue and dye and the tenant be in arerage of the
said rent and service in the time of the fadyr of the issue and not in yt time
of the issue / he may not distrayne for the areragis in the time of his fader /
and he shall have no another recouere agayns ye tenant or any other for that yt
sych aduau^tage is gyffyn by the law to ye tenant.

And note well that rent service is / to the which be longith fealte / but to
rent charge and rent sek longyth not fealte but yt be longyth to rent service
of common right.

Rent charge is where a man grauntyth certain rent going out of his landys or
tenementys to a another in fee simple or in fee tail or for term of life by
deed upon condycionn that at what day that the rent be behynd it shall be well
lawful to the grant or his heirs or assynys to distreyn in the same landys
or tenementys.

And note well that if the rent be behynd it is well lawful to the grant by
eleccyon to have a wryt of annuite / or else he may distreyn / and if ye
dystresse be takyn a gayns his will fro him and he was never seysyd before he
has no recouere but by writ of rescous / for ye distress furst takyn gyffyth
not to him seisi but if he hap ye re^t before for if he were seisyd of ye rent
before and after ye re^t be behynd and he distreyn and rescous to hi^ be mad he
shall have assise or a writ of rescous

And note well that in every assise of rent charge and a^nuall rent or in a writ
of a^nuite it behouyth to him that bryngyth the wryt to show forth an
especialte / or else he shall not maytayne assyse / but in a mordau^cest our
or formedou^ in the descender or other wryttes in the which title is gyffyn or
co^prisid of rent charge or anuell rent it nedyth not to show especialte.

And note well that if a man grant a rent charge to a another / and the grant
purchase the halff of the land whereof the rent is going out / all the rent is
extynct and if the grant relesse to the grauntour parcel of the rent / yet
all the rent is not extynct / But in rent service not withstondyng that the
lord has purchaysyd the halff of the land whereof the rent is going out / yet
the rent is not extynct but for the halff / and the cause is of the dyuersite
for that that rent service may be seueryd to one parson but not rent charge.

And note well that if rent charge be grauntyd to two ioyntenauntys and the one
relesse / yet the other shall have the halff of the rent / and also if one
purchase the halff of the land whereof the rent is going out the other shall
have the halff of the rent of his companion / And if the dissersour charge the
land to a stranger and the disseyse bring assise and recover the charge is
defetyd / But if he yt has right charge the land and a stranger feign a false
accion agayns him and recover by defaute / the charge abydyth.

And note well that in case that purparte be between two perceners and more land
be alottyd to one than to the other and she that has more of ye land chargyth
her land to ye other and she happyth ye rent she shall maynteyn assise without
especialte / and if the grant have it in fee simple or in fee tail and has
issue and dies / if the issue bring a formedone or assysc of mordauncestour he
shall never be chargid to show an especialte.

Rent sek is where a man holdith of me by homage fealte and other service
yeldyng to me a certain rent by the year and I grant this rent to a another
reseruyng to me the service.

And not well that in rent sek if a man be seisid of the rent / and the rent be
behynd he may not distreyn but he shall have assise of novel disseysyn.

And note well that if rent sek be grauntyd to a man and to his heyrys and the
re^t be behynd and ye grau^tour dye the heir may not distrayne nor shall not
recover the areragis of the time of his fadyr as it is be fore said of rent
service.

And in the same manner it is to sey of re^t charg or anuell rent / But in all
these rentes before said the heyr may have for the areragis in his own time
sych advantage as his fadyr had in his life.

And note well that in rent sek if a man be not seisid of the rent and it be
behynd he is without recover for that that it was his own folly at the beginning
when the rent was grauntyd to him or reseruyd that he took not seisin of ye
rent as a penny or. ii. d.

And note well yt a man may not have a cessa uit per biennum or a another wryt
of enter sur cessauit for no rent sek behynd by. ii. years but they may all
only for re^t service as it apperyth in the statute.

And note well yt in rent sek it behouyth for him that sewith for the rent sek
for to show a deed to the tenant or else ye tenant shall not be chargyd of ye
rent but where the rent sek was rent service before / as in this case / lord
mesne and tenant and every of them holdyth of other by homage fealte and. x. s.
of re^t / ye lord peramount purchaysith ye lands or tenementes of ye tenant /
all the signioury of the mesne but ye rent is extynet / And for this cause this
rent is become rent sek / and ye rent service chau^gyd / for he may not
distreyn for this rent / and i this case he ye demau^ dith the re^t shall never
be chargyd to show a deed / also in a wryt of mordauncestour ayle nor besayle
of rent sek it nedyth not to show a specyalte for y^ y^ these wryttes of
possession do comprehend a title within them selff that is to say that the
auncestour was seized of the same rent and continuyd his possession by cause of
which seysyn the law supposith that it is also auerable by the country tamen
quere for some suppose that yt be houyth of nessite to show forth a deed for
that that rent sek is a thing agayns common right as well as rent charge / But
in assise of novel disseysyn and in a wryt of enter sur disseysyn brought of
rent sek it behouyth of necessite to show forth a deed for vat that rent sek is
a thing agayns common right except in ye case before said where it was rent
service before

And assise of novel dissyesyn a wryt or enter sur cessaunt conteyn with in
them no title but suppose a disseisyn to be don to the playntyff and of the
inte^dement of the law the disseysyn gyffyth no cause of auerment agayns common
right but of necessite it be houyth to show forth a deed

Sewte service is to come to the court of three wekys to. iii. wekes by the hole
year and for that a man shall be distreynyd and not amercyed.

Sewte real is to come to the court of the let / and that is not but. ii.
tymys in the year and for that a man shall be amercyed and not dystraynyd.
