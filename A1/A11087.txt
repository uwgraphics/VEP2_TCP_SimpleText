TO THE RIGHT Honorable my singular good Lord and Master, the Earl of
Lennox, David Rowland wishes a happy increase of Honor and years in this
life.
COnsideringe with my self (right Honorable and my singular good Lord) how
in respect of time well employed, no Jewel can be thought precious or
comparable, nor any thing more unpleasant to the virtuous mind, then the
remembrance of the evil and idle bestowyng thereof: I have thought good as well
for the auoidinge of Idelnesse (Mother of all vice) as also, for the zeal I
bear to this my native Country, to select these Sentences, varied many ways,
out of the Jtalian Author Entitled in that toungue Specchio de la lingua
Latina, and to English them al under the name of A Comfortable aid for
Scholars, which I have chiefly done in the favor of your Lordshippes noble
and toward Son, trustinge that by healpe hereof, he may more easily attain
to a further knowledge in that tongue: wherein as your honor is most desirous
he should profit, so, (thanks be to God) he is now already well entered: and
now that I have finished my labor, supposinge some young studientes may (if
they will) reap commodity thereby, and that being published, it may rather do
good, then harm: J have presumed thus rudely and simply to set forth the
same, determinyng for diverse considerations to bestow it (being the first
fruits of my travel) rather upon your Honor (as duty binds me, being my
good Lord and Master) then upon any other person: because I know none whom
I thought would more favorably accept my doing, nor who of duty might more
justly claim the same then your Lordship, unto whom I do now most humbly
dedicate this my travel, as a token of my true and affectionate service,
meaning hereby outwardely to show, what the inward desire is willinge to
do, if power and ability were thereunto correspondent. And now, for full
accomplishment and perfection of this mean work, rests no more behind but
your Lordshippes Honorable favor to protect the same, which J most humbly
beseech you to receive into your noble tuition, as well to give countinance and
credit to the same, as also going forth in your Lordships name, to be an
occasion utterly to depresse the malignant and sclaunderous tongues of the
simple and unlearned sort: against whose injuries neither good will nor
labor is able to with stand, but only the maintenance of my cause by your
Honorable protection thereof, pleasinge to accept in good part this poor
attempt of mine, which doing your Lordshippe shall hereafter encourage me to
bestow my travel in matters of greater importance, and to acknowledge my
self much bound to your Honor, unto whom I now most humbly submit my self
and my doings, besechynge Almighty God favorably to defend, and govern your
Lordship in this life, and after that, mercifully to conduct you to the
unspeakable joys of his kingdom. Anno 1568. The.16. of May.

By your L. most humble and bounden Servant David Rowland.
