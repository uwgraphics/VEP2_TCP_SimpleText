THE EXAMINATION OF THE ACCIDENCE BY QVESTIONS AND Answers, wherein the
accidents of the eight parts of speech are familiarly handled and all
difficulties in the same arising explained.

Whereby young scholars may in shorter time learn to understand, and masters
with more ease, and better success teach the principles of the Accidence, then
it usually happens.

Set forth by T. C.

Scientia non habet inimicum praeter ignorantem.

LONDON Imprinted by John Norton, Printer to the Kings Majesty in Latin,
Greek, and Hebrew. 1606.
