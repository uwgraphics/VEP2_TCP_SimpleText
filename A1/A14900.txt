To the right worshipful his Master Edward Darcye Esquier, Groom of her
Majesty's privy Chamber, Thomas Weelkes wishes all heavenly joys whatsoever.
RIght worshipful, it is no small comfort the Music professors conceive,
when they consider the ever misdeeming multitude to brand them with infamy,
whom the most Honorable spirits have always honored: and although poverty
has debarred them their fellow arts men's company, yet nature has set their
better part at liberty, to delight them that love Music. Amongst so many
worthy men daily laboring to call home again the banished Philomele, whose
purest blood the impure Minstralsie has stained, I must presume to remember
one of your worships least labors, (your greater deeds greater men must seek
to requite) the entertaining into your service the least proficient in Music,
who with all dutiful obseruancie, humbly commend my poor labors to your
worships protection. My years yet vnripened, and this work not a little
hastened, cannot promise any choice notes of Music, yet notwithstanding I
presume that gentleness which accepts my service, will never reject the labors
of his servant: Thus humbly taking my leave, I leave these to your worships
favors, and you to the keeping of him that best can keep you.

Your worships servant. Thomas Weelkes.

TENOR. BALLETTS AND MADRIGALS TO five voices, with one to 6. voices: newly
published BY Thomas Weelkes.

IN LONDON PRINTED BY THOMAS ESTE, the assign of William Barley. 1608.

To the right worshipful his Master Edward Darcye Esquier, Groom of her
Majesty's privy Chamber, Thomas Weelkes wishes all heavenly joys whatsoever.
RIght worshipful, it is no small comfort the Music professors conceive,
when they consider the ever misdeeming multitude to brand them with infamy,
whom the most Honorable spirits have always honored: and although poverty
has debarred them their fellow arts men's company, yet nature has set their
better part at liberty, to delight them that love Music. Amongst so many
worthy men daily laboring to call home again the banished Philomele, whose
purest blood the impure Minstralsie has stained, I must presume to remember
one of your worships least labors, (your greater deeds greater men must seek
to requite) the entertaining into your service the least proficient in Music,
who with all dutiful obseruancie, humbly commend my poor labors to your
worships protection. My years yet vnripened, and this work not a little
hastened, cannot promise any choice notes of Music, yet notwithstanding I
presume that gentleness which accepts my service, will never reject the labors
of his servant: Thus humbly taking my leave, I leave these to your worships
favors, and you to the keeping of him that best can keep you.

Your worships servant. Thomas Weelkes.

IN LONDON PRINTED BY THOMAS ESTE, the assign of William Barley. 1608.

To the right worshipful his Master Edward Darcye Esquier, Groom of her
Majesty's privy Chamber, Thomas Weelkes wishes all heavenly joys whatsoever.
RIght worshipful, it is no small comfort the Music professors conceive,
when they consider the ever misdeeming multitude to brand them with infamy,
whom the most Honorable spirits have always honored: and although poverty
has debarred them their fellow arts men's company, yet nature has set their
better part at liberty, to delight them that love Music. Amongst so many
worthy men daily laboring to call home again the banished Philomele, whose
purest blood the impure Minstralsie has stained, I must presume to remember
one of your worships least labors, (your greater deeds greater men must seek
to requite) the entertaining into your service the least proficient in Music,
who with all dutiful obseruancie, humbly commend my poor labors to your
worships protection. My years yet vnripened, and this work not a little
hastened, cannot promise any choice notes of Music, yet notwithstanding I
presume that gentleness which accepts my service, will never reject the labors
of his servant: Thus humbly taking my leave, I leave these to your worships
favors, and you to the keeping of him that best can keep you.

Your worships servant. Thomas Weelkes.

BASSVS. BALLETTS AND MADRIGALS TO five voices, with one to 6. voices: newly
published BY Thomas Weelkes.

IN LONDON PRINTED BY THOMAS ESTE, the assign of William Barley. 1608.

To the right worshipful his Master Edward Darcye Esquier, Groom of her
Majesty's privy Chamber, Thomas Weelkes wishes all heavenly joys whatsoever.
RIght worshipful, it is no small comfort the Music professors conceive,
when they consider the ever misdeeming multitude to brand them with infamy,
whom the most Honorable spirits have always honored: and although poverty
has debarred them their fellow arts men's company, yet nature has set their
better part at liberty, to delight them that love Music. Amongst so many
worthy men daily laboring to call home again the banished Philomele, whose
purest blood the impure Minstralsie has stained, I must presume to remember
one of your worships least labors, (your greater deeds greater men must seek
to requite) the entertaining into your service the least proficient in Music,
who with all dutiful obseruancie, humbly commend my poor labors to your
worships protection. My years yet vnripened, and this work not a little
hastened, cannot promise any choice notes of Music, yet notwithstanding I
presume that gentleness which accepts my service, will never reject the labors
of his servant: Thus humbly taking my leave, I leave these to your worships
favors, and you to the keeping of him that best can keep you.

Your worships servant. Thomas Weelkes.
