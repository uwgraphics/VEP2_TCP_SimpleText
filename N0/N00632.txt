By His Excellency Coll. Benjamin Fletcher, Captain General and Governor in
Chief of His Majesty's Province of New York, etc. A PROCLAMATION
WHEREAS I have this Day received Information from the Right Honorable the
Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council, bearing Date the 20th Day of April last
past, That the French were making Preparations by Shipping, and otherwise, for
an Attempt on some of His Majesty's Plantations in America, and have put on
board a considerable Quanity of Arms for that purpose. And whereas the said
Right Honorable the Lords of His Majesty's Privy Council have directed me,
That all His Majesty's Loving Subjects within this Province be put in a
Readiness and Posture of Defense, and have given Assurance That all speedy Care
shall be taken to send Shipping, and other Force to our Relief and Succor.
These are therefore to Require all Officers, Civil and Military, and all
Persons within this Province to be vigilant and careful in having their Arms
well fited, and being well furnished with Ammunition, etc. And if any Person or
Persons whatsoever shall discover any Number of Ships or other Vessels upon
this Coast, upwards of Three at one Time they are hereby Required to bring the
Intelligence thereof forth^^^^ to New York to Me, or in my Absence to some of
His Majesty's Council for this Province, (...) will answer the contrary ^t
their highest Peril.

Given at Fort William Henry the second Day of August, in the Eighth Year of
the Reign of our Sovereign Lord, WILLIAM the Third, by the Grace of God, King
of (...), Scotland, France and Ireland, Defender of the Faith, Annoque Domini
1696. Ben. Fletcher.

God Save the KING
Printed by William Bradford, Printer to the Kings Excellent Majesty at the
Bible in New York 1696.
