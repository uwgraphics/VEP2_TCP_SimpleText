or them so dying or being removed, shall be paid to the Day of such Death or
Removal respectively. And to the Intent that you our said Commissioners may be
the better enabled to attend the Execution of this our Commission, and the
trust hereby reposed in you, and may not be withdrawn therefrom, Our Will and
Pleasure is, and we do hereby grant, declare, and ordain, That whilst this our
Commission shall continue in Force, you our said Commissioners and all Officers
of the Customs, in or for the Management of our said Revenues within the Limits
aforesaid, shall not be compellable to serve on any Jury, or to appear or serve
at any Sessions, or to serve any Parish or other public Office whatsoever,
Civil or Military, hereby requiring and commanding all Mayors, Provosts,
Sheriffs, Stuarts, Bailiffs of Royalities and Royalties, Justices of the Peace,
Bailiffs, Constables and all other our Officers and Subjects whomsoever whom it
shall or may concern, that they and every of them do take Notice of this our
Royal Pleasure, as they will answer the contrary at their utmost Perils.
Lastly, we Will, and by these Presents do grant to the Commissioners hereby
constituted, that these our Letters Patent, or the Inrolment thereof, shall be
and remain in all Things firm, valid and effectual in the Law, any want of
Recital or any Misrecital, Omission, Imperfection, Defect, Matter, Cause or
Thing whatsoever, in any wise notwithstanding.

In Witness whereof we have caused these our Letters to be made Patent: Witness
Ourself at Westminster, the Eighth Day of September. In the seventh Year of our
Reign. By Writ of Privy Seal, YORK and YORK.
Entered in the Office of His Majesty's Treasury, the Fourteenth Day of
September, 1767.

F. REYNOLDS.


A true Copy, as on Record in the Secretary's-Office,

Attest. JOHN COTTON, D. Sec'ry.
