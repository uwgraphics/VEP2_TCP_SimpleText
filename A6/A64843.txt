A DECLARATION Of the Officers and Company of Seamen aboard his Majesty's
Ships, the Constant Reformation, the Convertine, the Swallow, the Antelope,
the Satisfaction, the Hynd, The Roebuck, the Crescent, the Pelican, the
Blackmore Lady, lately rescued for his Majesty's service: with an Invitation to
the rest of the fleet, and their Brethren on land, to join with them in their
just undertakings.

In 't Iaer 1648.

Even Verclaringhe vande Officieren end Compagnie van het Zee-Volck, often
Matrosen op Syne Majesteyts Schepen; Ghenoemt de Standifastighe Reformatie, de
Gordine, de Swaluwe, de Anteloop, de Satisfactie, 't Heart, de Roebuck, de
Croissant often Halve Maen, den Pelican, de Ioffrow Morinne, voor deses bestolt
tot Syne Majesteyts Dienst. Meteen aenlockinghe vande rest vande Vloot, end
derselve Broederen, aen Landt zijnde, om met hun te voegen in derselve
gerechti^h voornemen.
WY hebben overlang met groote patientie verwacht even geluckich eynde van onse
verderflijcke scheuringhe, door middel van even Personel-Tractaet, so menichmael
op-ghedrongen, door onse Broederen in Scotlandt, end ernstelijck begeert,
vande Generaliteyt van het Coninckrijcke, als blijckt by de verscheydene
Petitien vande Respective Graeffchappen, often Provintien, tot dien eynde
onlanghs ghedaen: Maer wy bevinden by volle experiencie, dat de Independerende
Party, in plaets van d'appliceringhe van hun selven, tot het bestellen end
veraccorderen der differentien, achtervolgens hun publijckelijck ghegeven,
beloften aen het gantsche Conincrijcke (maer seer schandelijcken ghevioleert in
haer Voor-stellinghen, in it Ledger), hebben in hare Handen ghenomen, end met
Garnisoen beset al de sterke plaetsen van't Coninckrijck, over-loopende,
ontwapenende end plunderende het platte Landt, als often het even
gheconquesteerdt Volck ware. End dit alles om geen ander oorsaecke, als dat
het Volck in eene ootmoedighe maniere, haer begeerte om Vrede te hebben,
gheopenbaert had, end even eynde te hebben van dese swear verdruckinghen, die
op hun laghen, door de arbitraire macht van Commissien end de ongerechtighe
continuatie vande Accysen end Land-schattinghen: Naer de gantsche
verstroyinghe van des Coninckx Legher, van welcker, boeten often bestraffinghen
end compositien, Sy gelts ghenoech kreghen, om voldaen te hebben het meeste
deel der schulden van het Coninckrijcke. End van ghelijcken siende dat de
Macht end den Staet vande Scheeps-Vloot in sodanige handen waren ghestelt, die
niet alleen Vyanden waren, vanden Coninck end het Coninckrijck, maer oock
vande Monarchy selfs: Dat den Still vande Commissien ter See, was onlangs
verandert, latende des Coninckx naem daer uyt, end alleen het Parliament
mentionerende end het Legher. to Welck wy verstonden te zyn even ontervinghe
van Sijne Majesteyt end Sijne Kinderen, dat wy geen bestelde maniere en hadden
van Gods Dienst t*Scheep, geen Avontmalen, weynich often geen Predicatien in
ghebruyck, als van ongeleerde end mechanique Persoonen, datter eenen aenslach
was om Lant-Soldaten op elck Schip te senden, om te vermeesteren en de to
overtreffen de Zee-luyden, dingen so gants contrary de oude Gewoonten end
Orderen ter Zee, als dat wy, ons dachten in Conscientie verbonden te zijn, om
yets te done tot weder-kryginghe von ons eygen Recht: De herstellinge vande
Religie, naer Gods Woort, end het Verbont so solemnelijcken ghemaeckt. d'Eer,
Behoudenisse end Vryheyt van Sijne Majesteyt: De Privilegie van 't Parliament:
End de Vryheyt vande Onderdanen. End niet langher ghemaeckt te zijn het
ongheluckige Instrument van d'ellenden vanden Coninck end Coninckrijck, tot
welcken tijdt God ons inden Sin gaf end onse herten encourage erden end onse
handen sterckten om Colonel Ramsborough af te setten van het Commandment vande
Vloote. Even Man van heel verderffelijck bestaen, beyde in Religie end Policy
end even bekent Vyant vanden Vrede, end van 't oude Gouvernement van dit
Coninckrijck, 't welck wy ghesworen hebben te mainteneren end te behouden, met
ons Life end Goederen. Begeerende all eerlijcke Zee-luyden niet langer tot
spot te zijn met woorde^, maer u al tsamen tot ons te voeghen end uwe handen
te slaen aen so even eerlijck Werck, waer van de Eere (naest God) u sal
ghegheven werden, end de Beloninghe buyten twyffel sal naer advenant zijn, in
dese Wereelt end inde toekomende. Wy zijn langh gedruckt gheweest, inden name
vanden Coninck doch teghens sijnen will, end tot sijn schade. Laet ons hem nu
eenen Dienst done inder daet, in deser tijt van sijne grootste afflictie, daer
in God hem wonderlijcken vercloeckt end vermogent ghemaeckt heeft om uyt te
staen, end te verdraghen, end sal sonder twyfel ter rechter tijt, hem ons
weder geven end ons tot onse bekende Wetten end Vryheyt brenghen. Siet op den
Prince van Wales, staende Erffgenaem vande dry Croonen end Generalissimo
onder sijnen Conincklijcken Vader, over all Machten te Water end te Landt.
Siet op den Hertoch van Yorck Grooten Admirael van Englandt, zijnde Princen van
so groote hope en verwachtinge, dat de Wereelt dier ghelijcke niet en heeft,
ghekomen van het grootste Bloet in 't Christenri^ck, geallieert end bevrundet
met al de andre Buyten-Landtsche Potentaten, gheroepen tot de Scotsche Natie
end het meeste deel van d'onse, om het hare wederom te kryghen die daer geene
handt gehadt hebben inde ellenden des Coninckrijcks, end considereert by U
selven met wat Godlijcke often Menschen Wet, Sy uyt hare gherechtighe Possessie
konnen gestooten werden in Ballingschap, om daer in te leven als often het ware
vande aelmoessen van vremde. Comt end voecht U tot ons, end U en sal niets
ontbreken: Wy hebben goede Schepen, goede Herten, goede Handen, end dat boven
allen weert is, God end het Recht aen onse zyde. End den Almachtighen
Door-soecker van all Herten will ons so voorspoedighen in onse Voor-nemigen,
ghelijck wy voor-hebben de bevoorderinghe van sijn Glory tot het bestendighen
vande ware Protestante Religie, naer sijn heyligh Woort, de Eere, Vryheyt end
goet Recht van Sijne Majesteyt tot de continuatie, vande Privilegien van 't
Parliament end vande Liberteyt vanden Orderdaen door eene egale often gemeyne
wel-gegronded Vrede.

A Declaration of the Officers and Company of Seamen aboard his Majesty's Ships
the Constant Reformation, the Convertine, the Swallow, the Antelope, the
Satisfaction, the Hynd, the Roebuck, the Crescent, the Pelican, the Blackmore
Lady; lately rescued for his Majesty's service; with an Invitacion to the rest
of the fleet, and their Brethren on land to join with them in their just
undertakings.
WE have long expected with great impatience, an happy end of our wasting
divisions by means of a personal Treaty, so often pressed by our Brethren of
Scotland, and earnestly desired by the Generality of the Kingdom, as
appears by the late several Petitions of the respective Counties to that
purpose. But we find by sad experience that the Independent party, in steed
of applying themselves to the settlement and composure of differences,
according to their faith publicly given to the whole Kingdom (But most
shamefully violated in their proposalls of the Army) have seized into their
hands, and guarrisoned all the strong places of the Kingdom, overrunning,
disarming, and plundering the Country, as if it were a conquered Nation. And
all this for no other cause, But that they, in an humble way had manifested
their desire of peace, and to have an end of so heavy pressures, which lay
upon them, by the arbitrary power of Committees, and the unjust continuation
of the Excise and Land-taxes, after the utter defeat of the Kings party; from
whose fines and Compositions they drew money enough to have satisfied most part
of the debts of the Kingdom. And Seeing likewise, that the Power and Affairs of
the navy, were put into such hands, as were not only enemies to the King and
Kingedome, but even to Monarchy it self, that the style of Commissions at sea
were latly altered, leaving out the Kings name, and mentioning only the
Parliament and Army, which we understood to be a disinherison of his Majesty
and his childeren, that we had no settled form of divine worshipp, no
Communions, little or no preaching on board, but by illiterate and mechanique
persons, that there was a design of introducing land fouldiers into every ship
to master and overawe the sea men, things so contrary to the ancient Customs
and Orders of the Sea, that we thought our selves bound in conscience to do
something for the recovery of our own right, the reestablishment of religion
according to the word of God, and the covenant solemly taken, the honor,
preservation, and freedom of his Majesty, the Privilege of Parliament, and
the Liberty of the Subject, and not be made any longer the unhappy Instruments
of the misery of King and Kingedome, at which time God put it into our minds,
and encouraged our hearts, and strengthened our hands, to remove Colonel
Rainsborough from the command of the fleet, a man of most destructive
Principles both in Religion and Policy, and a known enemy to the peace and
ancient government of this Kingedome which we have sworn to uphold and mantayne
with our lives and fortunes, Desyreinge all honest seamen not to be deluded any
longer with words, but one and all, to join with us, and to put to their
hands to so honest a work, the glory whereof (next under God) will be
imputed to them, and the reward no question will be Proportionable, both in
this world, and the world to come. We have bin long pressed in the Kings
name, though against his will and interest, let us now do him some real
service in this time of his great affliction, which God has wonderfully
enabled him to bear, and will doubtless in his good time restore him to us,
and us to our known laws and libertyes. Look upon the Prince of Wales heir
apparent of the three crowns, and Generalissimo under his Royal father of all
forces by sea and land. Look upon the Duke of York Lord high Admiral of
England, Princes of so much hope and forwardnes, as the world has not the
like, extracted from the highest blood in Christendom, allied to, and
befreinded by all foreign Potentates, invited by the Scottish Nation, and by
the best part of ours to the recoveringe of their own, who have had no hand in
the miseryes of this Kingedome, and consider with your selves by what law of
God or man they can be thrust out of their just Possessions into exile, there
to live, as it were, upon the alms of strangers Come in and join with us, and
you shall want nothing: we have good ships, good hearts, good hands, and
which is worth all, God, and the law on our side. And the Almighty searcher of
all hearts so prosper us in our undertakings as we Intend the advancement of
his glory in the setlement of the true Protestant religion according to his
holy word, the honor freedom and just rightes of his Majesty, the
confirmation of the Privileges of Parliament, and of the liberty of the
subject, by an equal and well grounded Peace.
