TO THE READER.
THe vending and disposal of Books this Auctionary way, though heretofore a
Stranger, yet of late has so frequently obtained in the City of London, that
the known Method thereof may supersede any large or tedious Preface; only in
brief the Candid Reader is desired to take notice, that the Books mentioned in
the Ensuing Catalogue, are no Collection made by any private hand for Gain,
(which has been imputed to some Auctions, as a Reflection.) But were really
belonging to their Proprietors Deceased, mentioned in the Title Page, and by
the Directions of their respective Executors exposed to Sale.

The manner and Conditions of the Auction follow.
First, That having this Catalogue of the Books, and their Editions under their
several Heads and Numbers, it will be more easy for any Person of Quality,
Gentlemen or others, to Depute any one to Buy such Book or Books for them as
they shall desire, if their Occasions will not permit them to be present at the
Auction themselves.

Secondly, This Auction will begin on Monday the second day of December next
ensuing; At the Sign of the Harrow over against the College of Physicians in
Warwick Lane, at Nine a Clock in the Morning, and at Two in the Afternoon, and
so Continue day by day until all the Books are Sold; Wherefore it is desired*
that the Gentlemen or those Deputed by them, may be there precisely at the
Hours appointed, least they should miss the opportunity of Buying those Books
which either themselves, or their friends desire.

Thirdly, That he who bids most, is the Buyer; But if any manifest Difference
shall arise, that then the same Book or Books shall be forthwith exposed again
to Sale, and the highest bidder to have the same.

Fourthly, That the Buyers are desired to pay down at the Sale one shilling in
part of payment for all such books, as they shall buy at this Action; And the
residue of the Money is to be paid within one Month after the Sale, at the
place aforesaid, where the Book or Books so bought upon payment thereof, shall
be delivered.

Fifthly, That all the Books, according the Catalogue, are (forasmuch as is
known) Perfect; But if any of them appear to be otherwise before they be taken
away, the Buyer shall have his Choice of Taking or Leaving such Imperfect Book
or Books.

Lastly, All Persons who have a desire to see any, or all of these Books, and
to satisfy themselves in the Condition, and Editions of them, may please to
repair to the place aforesaid, from the 18th. day of this present November,
until the first Sale-day before mentioned, where constant attendance shall be
given for their Satisfaction, from the Hours of Eight in the Morning to Twelve
at Noon, and from One to Five in the Afternoon.

N. R
