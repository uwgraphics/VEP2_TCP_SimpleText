At a Common Counsel held on Tuesday, the 20th. day of August, 1650.
IT is Ordered by this Court of Common Council, FOOT Major. London.
that Thurseday next, the 22 of this present August, shall be set apart for
especial seeking of God, by Prayer and Fasting, for the Army which is gone
hence into Scotland: and Mr. Griffith, Mr. Sterry, Mr. Brooks, Mr. Feak, Mr.
Cardwell, Mr. Greenhill, Mr. Thomas Goodwin, Mr. Powell, Mr. Sedgwick, Mr. John
Sympson, and Mr. Sidrack Sympson, or any 4 of them, to be desired to be then
here, to pray with this Court, which is to meet in the Common Counsel Chamber,
at nine of the clock on Thurseday morning; And every Member of this Court is
especially desired to be present here that day; and in the close thereof, the
Court will subscribe, as God shall move each man, (and afterward the Members
of this Court will move the Inhabitants of their several Precincts, and every
Member of this Court that shall be then absent) for a free Contribution to be
sent to the poor Soldiers (in that Expedition) who have so freely adventured
their lives, for the safety of this City, and the whole Commonwealth.

Sadler.
