Thursday the Thirteenth of August, 1657. At the Council at White Hall.
HIs Highness the Lord Protector and His Privy Council, taking notice of the
Hand of God, which at this time is gone out against this Nation, in the present
Visitation by Sickness that is much spread over the Land, which calls upon the
People of this Nation to humble themselves in a solemn manner before the Lord,
and to seek his Face in reference thereunto; His Highness and the Council have
thought fit to set apart to Morrow Seven-night being Friday, the One and
twentieth day of this Month, for a Day of Solemn Fasting and Humiliation for
the ends aforesaid, to be observed within the Cities of London and Westminster,
and the late Lines of Communication, and Weekly Bills of Mortality, and all
other places in this Nation to which notice hereof shall come; not doubting but
the People of God in other parts of the Nation also will be forward in their
particular Congregations to a Duty so necessary at this time.

Hen: Scobell, Clerk of the Council.

LONDON: Printed by Henry Hills and John Field, Printers to His Highness the
Lord Protector. 1657.
