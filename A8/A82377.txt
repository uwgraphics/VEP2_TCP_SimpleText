AN ACT For Continuing Two former Acts touching ELECTIONS IN The City of London.
BE it Enacted by this present Parliament, and by the Authority thereof, That
one Act, Entitled, An Act disabling the Election of diverse persons to any
Office or place of Trust within the City of London, and the Votes of such
persons in such Elections: And also one other Act, Entitled, An Act concerning
the Election of Questmen, Constables, and all other subordinate Officers
whatsoever, within the City of London and Liberties thereof, be, and hereby are
Revived and Continued, and shall be, according to the true scope, intent and
meaning of the said several Acts, in all things observed, kept and executed,
for the space of One whole year, from the Eighteenth day of December, One
thousand six hundred and fifty.

Die Mercurii, 18 Decembr. 1650.
ORdered by the Parliament, That this Act be forthwith printed and published.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

London, Printed by Edward Husband and John Field, Printers to the Parliament
of England, 1650.
