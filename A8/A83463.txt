Thursday, February 23. 1659.
Resolved upon the Question by the Parliament,
That all the Militias in the respective Counties, and the Powers given to
them, be and are hereby revoked, and that the Levying of any Men, Moneys,
Horses or Arms be forborne: And this Vore be forthwith Printed and Published,
and sent down into the respective Counties, Cities and Boroughs; And that the
respective Members of the several Counties do send them into the several
Counties by the Post this Night.

THOMAS St. NICHOLAS,
Clerk of the Parliament.
LONDON, Printed by John Streater, and John Macock, Printers to the Parliament,
1659.
