APPOINTING JUDGES For the ADMIRALTY.
BE it Enacted by this present Parliament, and by the Authority thereof, That
John Godolphin Doctor of the Laws, and Charles George Cock Esquire be, and are
hereby Nominated, Constituted and Appointed Judges of the Admiralty. And the
said John Godolphin and Charles George Cock, are hereby Authorized, Empowered
and Required to Hear, Order, Determine, Adjudge and Decree, in all matters and
things, as Judges of the Admiralty, in as full, large and ample manner, as any
other Judge or Judges of the Admiralty at any time heretofore might or ought
lawfully to do; To have, hold, exercise and enjoy the said Office, or Place of
Judges of the Admiralty, until the Tenth day of December, and no longer.

Tuesday, July 19. 1659.
ORdered by the Parliament, That this Act be forthwith Printed and Published.

THO. St NICHOLAS Clerk of the Parliament.

London, Printed by JOHN FIELD, Printer to the Parliament. And are to be sold
at the seven Stars in Fleetstreet, over against Dunstans Church, 1659.
