Die Veneris, 6 Julii, 1649.
ORdered by the Commons assembled in Parliament, That Wednesday next come
three weeks be set apart and appointed for a day of public Fasting and
Humiliation, for seeking unto Almighty God in an especial manner, for his
blessing upon the Forces designed, and now going for the relief of Ireland, to
be observed in all Cities, Towns and places in England and Wales, besides the
Cities of London and Westminster: And that the Sheriffs of the several Counties
in England and Wales, do take care to disperse this Order unto the Ministers of
the several Churches aforesaid: And the Justices of peace in the several
counties, are required to take care for the due observation thereof; And that
the Ministers do publish this Order in their several Churches, on the Lords day
next before the day appointed for the due observation of the said Fast.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti'.

London, Printed for Edward Husband, Printer to the Parliament of England, July
7. 1649.
