AN ACT For continuing John Bradshaw Sergeant at Law, Chancellor of the Duchy
and County Palatine of Lancaster, and Bartholomew Hall Esquire; Attorney General of
the Duchy of Lancaster, and for continuing the Jurisdictions of the said
Duchy and County Palatine of Lancaster.
WHereas there is an Act of this present Parliament, Entitled, An Act for the
making of John Bradshaw Sergeant at Law, Chancellor of the Duchy and County
Palatine of Lancaster, and for authorizing the said Chancellor to pass a Patent
for constituting of Bartholomew Hall Esquire; Attorney General for the Duchy of
Lancaster: Now for continuance thereof, and of the Jurisdictions of the said
Duchy and County Palatine of Lancaster; Be it Enacted by this present
Parliament, and by the Authority thereof, That all the Offices, Places, Powers,
Jurisdictions and Authorities thereby given unto the said John Bradshaw, and
the said Bartholomew Hall therein named; as also the Jurisdictions of the said
Duchy and County Palatine, be continued until the First day of January, One
thousand six hundred fifty and two, Any Act or Order to the contrary
notwithstanding.

Thursday the First of April, 1652.
ORdered by the Parliament, That this Act be forthwith Printed and published.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

London, Printed by John Field, Printer to the Parliament of England. 1652.
