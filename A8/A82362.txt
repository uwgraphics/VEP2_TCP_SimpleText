For Continuance of a former Act For Settling the MILITIA OF THIS COMMONWEALTH.
BE it Ordained and Enacted by this present Parliament, and by Authority of the
same, That the Act, Entitled, An Act for Settling of the Militia of the
Commonwealth of ENGLAND, and all and every the Powers therein contained, shall
be in force and have continuance to all intents and purposes, until the First
day of May, One thousand six hundred fifty and one, Any thing in the said
former Act contained, or otherwise to the contrary notwithstanding.

Die Martis, 28 Januarii, 1650.
ORdered by the Parliament, That this Act be forthwith printed and published.

Hen: Scobell, Cleric. Parliamenti.

London, Printed by Edward Husband and John Field, Printers to the Parliament
of England, 1650.
